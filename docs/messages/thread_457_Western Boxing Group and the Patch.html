Western Boxing Group and the Patch

From: phinstarr | Date: 2006-02-10 22:35:40 UTC
Here is my history behind this patch endeavor. The LbNA group got way
too many posts that had nothing to do with the eastern part of NY
state. So I went to the LbNE group which worked out fine. Then in the
mean time the western New York group was formed and I was
like "YES!!!" At this point in time I decided that I would not begin
an eastern group because I did not believe there were enough boxers
interested. Also, I did not wish to split the state. Then I thought
the western NY group would eventually realize they would have to
become a state (discovering the boxers from central NY) I did not in
any of these cases decide to become a mediator of eastern or NY group
because in summer I do not have access to a computer (although that
will be changing this summer).

Although I knew the t-shirt design existed when I posted the patch
idea, I thought it was from a gather that I had attended not a logo
which represented the old western site.

I may be ruffling peoples' feathers here but I think the patch design
idea should be given a chance.

Though this is more then .02 worth, I firmly believe that newer
people of the NY state group should be allowed to have a say
in designing a patch/logo.

If Sprite's gets voted so be it.

BTW As of this date, I have no design to submit (though I may submit
one if time allows for me to draw one out).

I had tried to keep quiet about the postings but finally felt it
necessary to speak up as so many others felt it necessary to give an
opinion instead of just voting and let the vote speak for itself.

phynstar

From: Daina | Date: 2006-02-11 17:53:20 UTC
I understand what you're saying. You're saying you want to make sure
people get equal say in this logo, which makes plenty of sense.
However, if that's the case, then what you've said here and on your
poll is a little contradictory. You're saying we should have a say,
but then you're saying we shouldn't discuss this, we should just
vote. You also only put a vote out on the table, but then didn't make
it clear what we were really voting on. I personally voted yes at
first because I thought you were asking should the group have a patch
with maybe some other kind of design, just for the sake of having a
nifty patch. Once I (and apparently other people) realized the idea
was to change the logo we'd all previously voted on for LbNY, that's
when I think a lot of folks got upset. It was, however unintentional,
rather misleading.

The idea of just voting without knowing all the the facts seems a
little strange, too. In no way did you mention that we already HAVE a
logo that we voted on some time ago, even though my post way back
when stated that changing the logo to reflect our whole state was
exactly what I had in mind. To force a vote on the same topic to try
to get a different outcome is to disregard what we as a group had
already said we wanted (almost like yelling "do over!" in the middle
of a game). That's just not the democratic way. Even in congress, no
one just brings up a vote and says, "okay GO!" The merits of the
previous discussions on that topic are discussed to decide if a vote
is even necessary and if the consensus is that there's no need to
change what's already been established, then no time is wasted on a
revote.

Also, to say that newbies need a chance to voice their opinion is
prefectly fine, except for two things. First, when you muddle the
facts (not mentioning the original logo, saying we're voting on a
patch, not a logo), and you close off discussion about the facts ("to
give an opinion instead of just voting and let the vote speak for
itself") you don't give them a clear understanding of what they're
voting for, so you're not really getting a real vote. It's almost
like people in Buffalo voting for a Canadian president; sure, we live
nearby, but we have only a basic understanding of Canadian life and
politics (most of which is skewed by varying opinions). Second,
anytime you join a group, that group doesn't turn around and revote
on their established symbols and I don't think anyone who joins a
group would find that odd. Millions of people come to the US every
day, but we've kept the flag, the eagle and the national anthem for
over 200 years. If we as a group had to revote every time we had a
dozen new members, at some point, even today's newbies would feel
slighted in the process.

Now, if you're asking should we come up with some NEW symbols to add
to the ones we have (like do we add a magnetic ribbon to go with the
flag and do we create OTHER songs about America) then sure! And if
Lock Wench still wants to commemorate her gathering with something,
I'm all for buying something like that, too. But all this needs to be
made clear and needs to be discussed before being brought to the
table instead being forced on a very misinformed and/or biased public.

So, just to clarify, let's start over. Let's make sure everyone
understands first that we do already have a logo that was voted on so
that there's no confusion. Also, when I say logo, I mean a design
that the whole group uses to represent itself in public, so I'm using
the two interchangeably. I created the logo on the back of the t-
shirt with that in mind when people felt like the two groups should
merge.

Then I'd ask this: What are you (Phynstar) specifically looking for
as an outcome to this patch question? First, are you trying to ask do
we want a patch or not or are you looking to replace the logo? If
it's about the patch, are you wanting to A) make a patch with any
kind of design to add to our set of LBNY stuff?, B) make a patch with
our current logo already on it. If it's about replacing the logo,
then we should ALL get to talk about the merits of doing so before
you ask us to vote so that everyone's got the same understanding and
can make up their mind in an educated way.

The S & H
