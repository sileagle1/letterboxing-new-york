Reports from the Road

From: Jen Z | Date: 2005-11-16 18:41:02 UTC
Well, we haven't been able to get out as much as many of you have, but we have some fun finds to report. I'm happy to report that two of my greyhound buddies, now "Budgie" and "Artichoke", :o) became curious about boxing, accompanied me on the Chauvet Cave series at Jamesville, and subsequently became HOOKED! Yeah, two new boxers in the area and both are already carving their first series to hide! :o)  We had a fun filled morning get-together as they carved their first signature stamps this weekend. Then --greyhounds and all!-- we went to Green Lakes and searched for/found Tom's Letterbox, Dalliance, and Noodle in a Basket.
 
I am not sure if Artichoke and Budgie have signed up for this board yet, but watch out for them! They are TOTALLY hooked, LOL! Budgie has already bought out all the Mastercarve at the SU bookstore and she and Artichoke are brimming with ideas for series! Hee hee!
 
At any rate, in the past two weeks I have finished finding all the Lascaux cave series, did the Chauvet Cave series, and did the three boxes aforementioned at Green Lakes. I have to say... Dalliance was a blast, written in WW2 code! VERY interesting and so fun!
 
This weekend --weather depending-- Budgie, Artichoke, me, and our gaggle of 800 or so greyhounds are looking forward to letterboxing in the Syracuse and Oswego areas... make hay before the snow flies, eh? It will be very sad once winter comes and we cannot box anymore! :o(

Re: Reports from the Road

From: thespiritofmrpigeon | Date: 2005-11-17 17:00:45 UTC
Yes, HDM and the bird of a different color should still be in place
for this weekend. I fixed the clues a little bit for that one that
you're missing (you weren't the only one who got tripped up on that
one!), so it should be a little easier now. Make sure you've got the
most recent clues, since one of the boxes had to be moved to a new
location since the last time you were there. Hmmm.... Sunday you
say...hmmmm.... While you're in town, I recommend picking up the
two boxes that are over at the Fort, if you haven't done so already.
They're pretty quick and worth the trip! I'll have to pull them
pretty soon or else they'll be swimming up to Canada!

As far as pulling boxes for the winter.... I do it. I plan to pull
all of them. I don't want to go letterboxing in the snow! I'll be
honest, I've never been out boxing in the snow before, but I can't
imagine how one can find something out there, especially with the
incredible snowfall that we get around here. Not to mention with all
the Lake Effect snow that Oswego county gets, so many trees fall
down, signs disappear, trails change. All of your clues to a
letterbox could be completely incorrect after a harsh winter has
changed the landscape. I like to take my boxes in at the last
opportunity, work on them during the winter (I've recently started
making logbooks, so perhaps I'll replace older ones with newer ones)
and then replace them in the spring, checking my clues to make sure
they're still accurate.

Just my $0.02.

Craftymouse
