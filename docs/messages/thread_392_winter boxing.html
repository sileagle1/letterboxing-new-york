Re: winter boxing

From: e_stuckwisch | Date: 2005-11-06 16:35:16 UTC
Thanks for the heads up! I usually find the Atlas Quest message
boards overwhelming, but your subject line got me right to the thread
in question. That's why I like this board, besides being full of
people I plan to meet one day, it's of a size I can keep up with.

Your point about tracks in the snow exposing boxes was one of the
things I was wondering about. It could be fun filling a whole park
with misleading tracks to disguise the real ones!

I think I'll be letterboxing this winter (my first) and from what I
read enough people in the area keep looking that I think I will keep
planting. I'm still working on the urban plants, it will be
interesting to see whether they're easier or harder than more rural
plants to find in the snow!

Still working on that personal traveller! I was going to box today,
but it just got really nasty out in the last hour, lots of wind and
rain here in downtown Bflo, and I swear the wind actually carried the
smell of the general mills plant out in the outer harbor all the way
over here. I recognized the smell from when I got the times beach
box a while ago. A great location, for those of you who haven't
gotten it yet!

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-11-09 14:16:35 UTC
I am happy to see there plenty of crazy winter hardy boxers out there.
The Desperately Seeking Sun event is up and running on atlasquest and
people are signing up! I will be posting more info as we get closer to
the event, so stay tuned. The new "I Love NY HH Hostel" has been
getting a lot of visitors too, so be sure to stop by and swap your HH's
when you are traveling this Thanksgiving holiday.

In the meantime, imagine my surprise as I was working the polls last
night? (I am an election inspector) I looked up as someone called
out "Lock Wench?" It was none other than SpudHund and DarthTater. So
cool to realize how close these boxers live to me! Our Syracuse Mayor
squeaked by his challenger...barely. No word on his slant of "boxing
issues."

I have set aside my boxing hat and pulled on my film fest hat as I am
knee deep in grant applications, but love to read of others' adventures
boxing in our beautiful NY state. So cough it up...what are you all up
to?

Lock Wench
