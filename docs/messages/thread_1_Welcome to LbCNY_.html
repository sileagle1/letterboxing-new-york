From: paulonthepark | Date: 2004-08-22 02:14:21 UTC
I hope that having this group to support letterboxing in Central NY
will make this come true:

More Letterboxers = More Letterboxes = More Fun!

Although I'm almost 3,000 miles away in San Francisco, I saw that
there WAS interest in letterboxing in the area, because within a day
or two of planting the 1st Oneida County letterboxes, people were
stamping in, and I began to receive e-mails thanking me for planting
the boxes.

I hope you aren't waiting for me to return with more boxes... because
if you each plant a box or two or three, it's going to become much
more fun -- and quickly.

I'm fairly new to letterboxing myself, having started this past
spring. I jumped in, feet first, and started planting AND hunting at
the same time. The Northern California Letterboxing group dubbed
me "Rookie of the Year". When I began, there were only a total of 7
boxes in the city of San Francisco itself. As I started planting
more, people joined in, and now there are over 30 boxes -- and all
within just a few months!

The boxes I hid in NY are simple, and I was assisted by my great
nephews and their friends. Take a look at some of my notorious San
Francisco boxes (go to www.letterboxing.org and find the clues for my
Vertigo #1 box, based on the Hitchcock film, in San Francisco). That
was my first planted box. I get a lot of fun e-mails from people who
have hunted for that box and others. Another favorite is Nepenthe,
based on Greek mythology. If you come to SF, bring youre letterboxing
gear!

The sky's the limit when it comes to creative ideas for letterboxes.
I now have what may have been the first underwater (yep! submerged)
letterbox, based on the theme of Atlantis. My "Yes, Noe!" box takes
you on a simple neighborhood garden tour, using only distorted photos
as clues. I'm now putting together a box that has less than 10 words
for clues (people joke that my clues are very word-y hahahaha!)

Go ahead and plant a letterbox to share something that you find
interesting, special, or memorable with others -- and have fun doing
it!

I have some excellent contacts who are very experienced letterboxers
and give great advice. If you have a question or need help, we'll get
you an answer.

I'm going to have my niece, Deidre, join me as co-moderator for this
group -- she's local and lives in the "Place of the Nettles".

Paul in SF

From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2004-09-30 19:48:55 UTC
We have that in common: word-y clues. I recently took that challenge of
brevity, and created my "Star-crossed" box:
http://home.earthlink.net/~pgonyea/star-crossed.htm

The 9 words in the box get people to the location in San Francisco, and the
rest are the specifics for where the box lies. I was content with my number
count, and people are able to find it, once they solve the words in the box.

It was fun - now back to my long narrative style:
http://home.earthlink.net/~pgonyea/vertigo1.htm and
http://home.earthlink.net/~pgonyea/yes-noe.htm , as examples.
