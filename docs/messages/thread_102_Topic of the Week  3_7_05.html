> Do you have any carving tips to share?

My carving tip is nice and sharp with a redesigned cutting angle,
pictured in the "Photos" section under "catbead's carving nibs". It's
a Speedball #1 cutter with the business end reground and resharpened.

It's what I use to cut around all the edges first, I can see where I'm
going (unlike the regular #1 cutter), and it makes nice tight turns.

Another thing is that I don't use holders for my cutting nibs.
Holding just the nib in my fingers gives me complete control, it
becomes an extension of my hand. To make it comfortable to hold,
masking tape was wrapped around the other end a few times to act as a
pad and grip.
