Kid-friendly usually means under 2 miles in round-trip length (though
people with really young kids would probably say under 1 mile), fairly
level terrain and free of hazards such as steep cliffs at trailside,
water crossings or boulder hopping. You might try looking at the
trails from a parent's viewpoint when you plant the boxes and see if
there are any elements that would make them concerned about their
little ones' safety when you're deciding if a particular box is
kid-friendly or not.

Pet-friendly usually means a location where pets are allowed. Include
information about any leash restrictions, if you can find that out.

Stroller-friendly usually means a paved path with relatively little
elevation change, something a regular (not jogging) stroller could be
pushed along without much difficulty. Stroller-friendly often
translates to wheelchair-friendly, which can be helpful for some to
know as well. If you do use the "wheelchair-friendly" designation,
just be sure to note in the clues if the box itself is off the main
path and not accessible to someone in a wheelchair.

It's very sweet of you to think of including this information,
especially for people who like to box with their families and/or pets.
