Re: doing what you want... My older sister looks great in hats, but won't
wear them. Why? "Because people in this village would think I look strange
in a hat", she says.

When I lived in Boston, there were certain fashion taboos around wearing
anything even remotely resembling a fetish item: vinyl, motorcycle jackets,
or leather pants for example.

Here in SF, I've seen an occasional person nearly or completely naked and
standing at a major intersection. People look, but don't make much about it.
We have HUGE annual fetish street fair with people wearing and doing things
that would get them thrown in jail anywhere else. It's about 8 blocks long,
and is one of the 2 biggest income generating events for this city.

When you first move here, you have to get used to how accepting of diverse
lifestyles and how politically correct this city is. Our fumbling president
would never dare to set foot here -- he knows better! He'd freak out!

But try to get a slice of pizza that's as good as New York's -- impossible!
And they call it New York Style Pizza!

Our art museums don't compare to the East Coast's because this state was
pioneer territory until after 1900, and by the time people collected art,
the good pieces were snatched up or very expensive.

Paul in SF
