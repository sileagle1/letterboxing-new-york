Enlightened Letterboxers don't buy Rubbermaid

From: Paul | Date: 2005-12-10 17:43:14 UTC
I just got my copy of the Human Rights Campaign's 'Buying for
Equality'report, which lists ratings for companies that may have
discriminatory policies or that don't support equality in the
workplace (or hiring) for gay and lesbians.

I regret to say that the Newell Rubbermaid Company scored a deplorable
29 out of 100 on their workplace equality practices.

I've learned that letterboxers tend to be very enlightened people, so
for those of you who know how important it is to support equality,
please don't buy Rubbermaid products, go for a different brand --
until Rubbermaid wakes up and changes their HR policies.

Thank you.
Paul in SF

Re: [LbNY] Re: Enlightened Letterboxers don't buy Rubbermaid

From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-12-10 20:11:14 UTC
Wow! What is it that makes letterboxers be so 'with it' and enlightened? For
the last 2 weeks I've helped raise cash and toys for Orphans with AIDS, and
my letterboxing friends were most generous. I got big boxes of toys from
LbNY members -- all the way across the country! Bravo!

LETTERBOXERS ARE ENLIGHTENED PEOPLE.

Yes, Ford Motors is trying very hard to maintain some sort of balance and
reasonableness. What I don't understand is why conservative religious groups
have an issue with Ford placing advertising in gay-oriented publications. No
one's making them read, or pay for those ads. Driving a Ford doesn't make a
person gay (at least not to my knowledge). I'd guess that Jesus would let
Ford advertise in The Advocate -- because he'd be busy helping the poor and
sick instead.

Gay men & lesbians have $610 billion dollars of buying power. Any smart
corporation that wanted to be profitable and do the right thing, would
advertise in a variety of publications to reach all target audiences.

I work for Charles Schwab & Co. and for a long, long time, Schwab has
recognized that our clients come from all sorts of backgrounds and represent
the diversity of the U.S. population. Our employees are representative of
that diversity, and equality is a key part of our HR policies. We scored a
perfect 100 in the Equality Buying Guide (under 'Money Matters').

What I see happening lately is insecurity around change itself... as society
changes and outdated modes of thinking fade away, people cling harder to
their religion so that they feel 'right' and safe. But equality isn't a
threat, and it isn't bringing about the downfall of civilization.

It's a PDF file. Adobe Acrobat required to read this document. Download and
install free Adobe Acrobat Reader.
