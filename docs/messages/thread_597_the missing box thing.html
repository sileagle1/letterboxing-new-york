the missing box thing

From: June | Date: 2006-10-01 06:33:09 UTC
I decided to change all my urban boxes to a 25 find count. I'll see how that works before I consider 50. I have a six box, now four, series in a non urban setting within the city and two went missing. I found the other four in the open. Besides the stamps, they had hand made logbooks that I put a lot of work into. One of the stamps that went missing was a pretty hard one and took a while to carve. I thought it was one of my best ones and I thought it would be safe. I don't think I will replace the two that are gone. That's it. I'm officially a Boxing Snob and proud of it. hah!

Re: [LbNY] the missing box thing

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-10-01 07:53:37 UTC
Well I will be a snob right with you, June. I had changed some of my boxes earlier in an effort to extend their life and limit their access to huge crowds.  I also changed some clues to make them slightly harder and made a couple into mystery boxes. The majority of my 200+ boxes are open to all, and anyone who wants to complain about that needs only to walk a mile in my shoes...or hiking boots, as it were. :)  Plant 200 boxes, work hard to maintain them, and if you still want to complain about mine; we can talk!

Urban boxes are a very special case and I don't think you are overreacting at all by putting some restrictions on them. If only to impress upon potential finders that these boxes require real stealth!
