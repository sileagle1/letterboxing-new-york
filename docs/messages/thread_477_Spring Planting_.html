Spring Planting?

From: Cyndie | Date: 2006-03-09 02:42:08 UTC
Hmm.."spring planting" means one thing to my gardening sister, but
quite another thing to me. It's time to begin planting the boxes I've
created and nurtured over the long cold winter.

Tomorrow, weather permitting, I am heading to the Cortland/Sempronius
area. I'd like to find Trailhoundz's Bear Swamp boxes and plant one
there myself..plus a few more in other nearby areas.

By far, the most boxes I have ready to go are for the southern and
central Adirondacks. The High Peaks region is beautiful and I really
enjoyed them in the past, but they are getting just too overrun with
tourists now for me to enjoy them as much. There are so many other
gorgeous areas of the Adirondacks..with cool swimming holes, beautiful
waterfalls and interesting caves and abandoned mines. Stay tuned! :)

Lock Wench

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-03-09 18:53:08 UTC
Yep, I planted one in Auburn. I know how fun it is to find new boxes in your own area! I just went and found one up at OCC that had been planted by J.A.P. and Seekher. I was surprised that I was only the second finder even though the box had been there almost a month!
 
Now I am glad I asked where you were planting, because I had planned one for Howland Island. Hadn't carved it yet, so I will look elsewhere. Four sounds like a good enough number for that place. I didn't make it to Bear Swamp as the rain was just pouring too much, but I will get mine there eventually. With 3,000 acres, it's not likely to get too crowded. I did manage to plant a couple at Lime Hollow, just south of Cortand, but the 2 boxes I have for Fillmore Glen will have to wait for a drier day.
 
I've been to Tahawus and agree it would be a great place for a box. I thought one was there already, but I may be mistaken. My favorite Adirondack area is the Siamese Pond Wilderness...especially Chimney Mt with it's rock climbers and cool caves. I've got probably 15 boxes sitting here for the Adirondacks, so get ready!
 
I know what you mean about replacing the missing ones. I've been slowly replacing mine as well. Slowly, because I am rethinking a couple of them. But it's my personal goal to never have more than 5% of my boxes missing at any given time. I think that's a decent goal.
 
I enjoyed the article about Wells and Pleasant Rowland, although I think the townspeople protest too much. I would be happy to have a millionaire take care of my town and insure it's financial success and beauty. She could be as picky as she wanted!  I would love to do your Wells series and I still have to replant my Honeymoon box down that way.
 
Btw, I stopped into a dollar store in Cortland and was pleased to find those big rubber erasers..the flat, rectangular ones with "For BIG Mistakes" written on the sides. Someone had posted that they really liked using these so I thought I would give them a try. They certainly were reasonable.....$1 for an eraser about 7 inches long and almost 2 inches wide..and about 3/8 inches thick. I'll let you know how they are.  I really am unhappy with the orange PZ Cut and wish they would switch back to the white stuff. The pink stuff is great but just so darn expensive.....
 
Lock Wench

From: Jen Z | Date: 2006-03-13 14:25:24 UTC
Yeah, I do need to rehide that first box. I also need to find newer (better!) hiding spots along the trails for my L & C Wildlife Adventures and Historic Auburn series, too. I've been mulling it over quite a bit lately, but there is such a VERY fine, VERY fine line between Letterboxing and Littering.
