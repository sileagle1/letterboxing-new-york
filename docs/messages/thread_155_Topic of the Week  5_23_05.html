Re: Topic of the Week 5/23/05

From: ducacj | Date: 2005-05-24 16:37:45 UTC
I am always curious how people get started letterboxing. I read about
it in a "If you're going to New England... here are some fun things to
try" Thought it sounded really cool. Went and checked out the
letterboxing.org website and saw that there were actually boxes where
we were going for vacation.

We did our first one outside Madison, WI. Found the box open and
plastic bags strewn around but no stamp. (Had we known what to look
for we would have probably tried to find everything for the box). Then
we went to Marinette, MI and round the Red Arrow Park box. We didn't
have a stamp or anything just found the box opened it up and
thought "Oh my god how cool".

We went immediately to Walmart bought a stamp, inkpad, and card stock
and went back to log in.

I spent 2 hours the next day at the library researching other boxes in
that area and we went out hunting. We haven't stopped since.

The Duca Family

Well, technically, WE did that. We actually researched a little online, but
didn't believe it. So, after a day at the doctor (the one who would later
inspire "Faith, Hope & Patience"!), we decided to cheer up by going to see
if this really existed. We found the Elba Onions' first few plants and were
first finders at 2 of them, as WELL as found 2 HH's! We didn't have a stamp
or anything, so we booked it to the store that week, bought ink, carved a
stamp and came back that weekend to stamp in!
