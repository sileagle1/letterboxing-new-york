Topic of the Week 1/9/05 What do you carry

From: AC | Date: 2005-01-10 21:55:21 UTC
The Bandits box with anywhere from 2 to 9 people, so what we carry depends on where we're going and how many are going. Personal stamps are just that... personal... everyone is responsible for their own stamp and inkpad, as well as water bottle.
 
We keep a crate in the bottom of the hall closet that has the staples which are carried year round:
 
Peanut Butter Crackers
Small Water bottles
Bug Spray
Compass
Flashlight
Extra Batteries
Large first aid kit
2 Pencils, pencil sharpener, sharpie and 2 ink pens
Ink pads
Wipes (for boxers, not stamps)
Anti-bacterial hand gel
Extra boxes (usually 2)
Extra baggies (stowed in the empty box)
Roll of Toilet Paper
Roll Paper towels
3 pairs of extra socks in various sizes
A NeatSheet (which gets transferred to the straps on the bottom of my pack, depending on weather. Best buy I ever made, it's been used to: eat on, as a rain poncho, sun shade and car seat protector for the baby bandit who fell in the creek.  When we're going out as a group, we just grab the whole thing and stick it in the trunk.
 
On me personally, I carry in my pack:
Compass
Map of where we're going
Clues for the box we're after (as well as a few others in the area JUST in case!)
Personal water bottle
Small first aid kit
Pen and pencil
Inkpad
My logbook
My stamp
Team Stamp
My rubberized garden gloves (sorry, don't do bugs)
Terry hand towel
Poncho
A Ziploc bag with a few sheets of toilet paper and a paper towed, folded flat.
 
In addition to all this, I have a special set of car keys that go in my pants pocket before we leave the house... This lesson was learned after once sitting my pack in the trunk to tie my boots,  while the keys were in the ignition (car was off). 
 
SLAM!... "Mom, should I lock the door for you?" (Child 1)
"NO!..."
"Oops..."
"Here Mommy, let me close..."
"NOOOOOOOOOOO!!!!!!!!"
..."the trunk for you." SLAM! (Child 2)
 
After that I had a set of letterboxing keys made.  Now the Owner's manual CLEARLY states it is not possible to lock the doors while the keys are in the ignition... Ford customer service swears if could not have happened.  But then I don't suppose the windshield wipers and door locks should turn on randomly either.
 
Oh, well...
 
See ya on the trails,
