Tales from the road

From: Jen Z | Date: 2005-06-26 20:10:58 UTC
This weekend was great.  Boxing Thursday, Saturday, and today! Woo hoo!  Anyone else get some boxes in this awfully hot weather?
 
Thursday the dogs and I drove to Syracuse and did "Straight Through to China", "Wonders of Onondaga Park", and "My Favorite Irish Things." On one of the searches we came across a very big and very dead Norway Rat. Ewww! (Not in the letterbox by the way, LOL!). Lucy and Clifford, my ex-racing greyhounds, were very excited upon the find! Gross! :o)
 
Saturday, my pal "Spudhund" (who got me addicted to letterboxing!) and I went out and did Taphophiles Unite (both St. Agnes and Oakwood), Your Ad Here, Work Drones (I got my first first-finders certificate, yeah!), Kaneeda, Just Passing Through NY, and Cairn.  Too hot for us to bring the greyhounds (my guys and her boy, Bailey) so we dragged her husband along, instead. He was a great navigator! :o)
 
Today Spudhund and I (and her hubby and the dogs) were up in northern Cayuga County for our greyhound walking club meet. We did the 5 Sterling Nature Center boxes... very cool! We then did our group walk with the club, and Spudhund, hubby, and some other greyhound people (potential converts, LOL!) went to do Three Mice and a Cat series of boxes. Unfortunately my girl Lucy, who will be 9 soon, was too whooped so we couldn't join them. Next time!
 
This afternoon, in front of the luscious fans, I am finishing up "Lucy and Clifford's Wildlife Adventure" series and we will be planting them tomorrow at the Cayuga Community College Nature Trail in Auburn. There are 9 stamps in this series. :o)
 
Hope everyone is keeping cool!

From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-06-27 01:16:44 UTC
Last week we FINALLY got out to do some late day boxing and nabbed
Lock Wench's "Give Life", and part 1 & 2 of of "I Found My Inner
Child at Inner Harbor". The day was getting late by the time we went
for #3 and happened upon some strange people near it and, in the
process, interrupted something....soooo we decided to come back
another time. Unsatified at not finishing, we nabbed "Cairn" on the
way home.... taking first finders as consolation. hehehe. :-)

Today we had some free time and finished "I Found My Inner Child at
Inner Harbor", and the only strange thing encountered was the dog
wondering why she couldn't chase the woodchucks. :-)

I don't know about anyone else, but personally, I think Syracuse has
gotten a lot more interesting since Lock Wench came on the scene. :-)

Cheers,
catbead
