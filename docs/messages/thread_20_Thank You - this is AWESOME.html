From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2004-10-02 21:05:22 UTC
I'm SO glad I started this group! I had a hunch it would take off and grow.
It's good to see so much enthusiasm, with people connecting with each other.
Now, I can picture there being a LbCNY gathering next summer (hopefully,
while I'm there), so everyone can meet face-to-face, and get dizzy
exchanging personal stamps! Now, I have two regional group affiliations:
LbCNY, my "vacation back home" group, and LbNCA (Northern California), my
home base group.

I think that this group should be open to anyone who plants or hunts for
boxes anywhere in or near Central NY (that would include all of NY, and
folks who come in from neighboring states). Some of you travel great
distances to hunt for letterboxes. And everyone has something to share, or
something to learn. Communities are great, but arbitrary boundaries are
merely artificial dividers.

Having said that, I'm now going to invite all of you in this LbCNY group to
participate in a long distance letterbox series. At some point, many people
come to San Francisco, either on business or vacation. A while ago, I posted
an invitation on LbNA, and I'll extend it to this group.

If and when you come to San Francisco, please join our letterbox series: "I
Left My Heart", and plant a letterbox somewhere here in the city that you
find is special to you. You can either call it "I Left My Heart # XX", and
take the next sequential number in the series listed on letterboxing.org,
OR, you can do as so0me people have already started. We have "I Left My
Tarts", "I Lost My Dart", and "I Left My Cart" so far, as a play on words.
So, if you come to San Francisco, be sure to wear some flowers in your hair,
and bring a letterbox to plant where you'll leave your heart (or cart or
tart, or...). Remember, these are urban letterboxes, and may need some
camouflaging. There's also the big green and forested Golden Gate Park,
which is much bigger than Central Park, and home to a number of happy boxes.
If you need some advice on disguising an urban box, e-mail me. I'm becoming
an expert quickly.

Welcome to the group, Songchick!
