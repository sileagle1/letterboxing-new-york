RE: [LbNY] Re: Stamp carving with a Dremel?!?!

From: AC | Date: 2005-10-16 13:02:51 UTC

I find that all the mediums eventually wrap themselves around the nibs.    All you really do with a Dremel is to remove areas of medium in areas.  It’s not good even with the finest of nibs for lines.  I’ve used it on the Pink stuff, the beige-y stuff, PZKut, and Plumber’s gasket and Mastercarve.  If you can get hold of one, I’d suggest having a flex shaft.  It allows you to have a pen or shovel like grip without you having to hold on to the entire weight of the tool in your hand.  It has to hang and will bind if you are working in a tight space, but it make use MUCH easier.

 

I only use it now when I need to create a large area that needs “texture” or sometimes to create a large area of negative space.  Although, I usually still wind up using one of the larger flat speedball nibs to clean it up as the Dremel makes a mess of your background and requires quite a bit of cleanup.  And I’m particular about the “neatness” of the stamp.  I can be far more productive with the Speedball, since the Dremel requires great care.  You accidentally drop the speedball on you work you MIGHT get a ding In your work… drop the Dremel and you might windup putting a hole in the table underneath J

 

I’d suggest using a heat transfer method, which seems to make PZKut smoother, more like Mastercarve.  If you need really fine lines, stick it in the freezer for a few minutes before carving. 

 

TT

The flex-shafts don’t control the speed.  The speed is generally controlled by a thumb wheel or slider right on the neck of the Dremel.  You have to be VERY careful when detailing with the thing… one slip up and you have a nice big eraser.
