Hello everyone!

I am a video productions student at Syracuse University. I came
across letterboxing by surfing the internet. For this next semester,
our class is putting together a couple of documentaries. All of them

From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-01-24 04:10:31 UTC
Well, seeing as how I have experience with video production as part of my
job, and I'm currently taking a class in documentaries, I should be able to
help you. I'm not as experienced with letterboxing as some veterans around
here, but I know enough to be a good resource. Anything other info you need
I can get from the experts for you.

If you want my help, let me know, and give me an idea of what info you need
for starters, OK? I do think it would make an interesting story, especially
since the information about letterboxing that has reached the general public
has only scratched the surface. Keep in mind that much of letterboxing's
appeal comes from the secrecy involved -- maybe you should keep that
idea/theme/mood as background for your documentary. Make it seem somewhat
like a mysterious activity (which it is).

BTW, I live in San Francisco, but I'm originally from Utica, and have
planted 5 letterboxes in Central NY (Utica-Rome).
