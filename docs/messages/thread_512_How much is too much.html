How much is too much

From: songchick | Date: 2006-05-08 02:10:04 UTC
Got an etiquette question for you all ...

Is there such a thing as having too many boxes in one location? I'm
working on my cemetary series (which has four boxes to date, and at
least four more in the process of being carved). I went to find
some new boxes today, which were located in a cemetary I wasn't
familiar with and would make the neatest addition to my series.

Here's my dilemna ... there is already a series of 3 boxes located
in the cemetary, as well as a fourth box planted by a different
letterboxer. It's a really big place, and I could hide another box
for my series easily without interfereing with the existing boxes.
But I don't want to step on toes by adding a fifth box. As long as
I found a location AWAY from the other 4 boxes, and not somewhere
that's disrespectful to those buried in the cemetary (and their
families), would that be acceptable?

There are other areas locally that are starting to "overlap" boxes
as well. Seems like good old Broome County is finally catching up
to the rest of NY!!! YAY!!!!

Songchick - I have been very happy to see all the boxes you've been
planting in New York State! I, for one, do not think we are in danger
of becoming another CT. The more, the merrier. If you are really
concerned about crowding another person's boxes, then I would email
them and tell them what you have in mind, and see if it bothers them.
If you receive no response after a week, I would go ahead and plant.
No one "OWNS" a location, so traditional etiquette is all that would
be required.

My friend Paul planted a box only a couple of feet above one of mine!
It didn't bother me in the least. I figure it brings more people to
both of our boxes.
