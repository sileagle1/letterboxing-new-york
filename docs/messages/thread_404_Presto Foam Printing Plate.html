Presto Foam Printing Plate

From: Paul | Date: 2005-11-19 19:50:42 UTC
The other day I stumbled upon a package of Presto Foam at the art &
craft supply store. My curiousity forced me to buy a pack of 12 foam
printing plate sheets -- they're cheap.

There are no instructions. What can anyone tell me about Presto Foam?
Has anyone tried using it instead of rubber stamp stuff?

Even though it's thin and breaks easily, it could be mounted -- or
used for event stamps with limited use -- or maybe it'd be great for
kids.

Thanks!
Paul in SF

(P.S. I posted this in LbNY instead of LbNCA because some folks here
explore and try lots of new things.)

Re: [LbNY] Presto Foam Printing Plate

From: Norasta | Date: 2005-11-20 14:41:52 UTC
I've used this stuff with kids and have created a few
Lboxes with it as well. Cuts with scissors - easy,
prints nicely. You have to plan on simple bold
designs. There was quite a long discussion about foam
stamps a while back... not sure which board.
Norasta

Re: [LbNY] Presto Foam Printing Plate

From: Norasta | Date: 2005-11-20 21:14:48 UTC
I used a small pair of scissors to do mine. I was
also able to "dent" the foam with a dried up ball
point pen. Both methods are limited in the amount of
detail you can do. You can't really carve the stuff,
tho it may be possible to get finer cuts with an
exacto. Let me know if you give it a try. Maybe we
can upload some pics....
