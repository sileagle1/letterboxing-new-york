From: songchick | Date: 2004-12-20 01:31:59 UTC
OK - here are my answers from both this week and last
week ... busy time of year here and haven't been able
to get online much

My trail name was actually given to me by one of my
students. Quite a few years back, one of the little
darlings told me "for a teacher, you're a pretty cool
chick". Then another one piped up "yep, a SONG
CHICK!!" Considering the fact that I'm a music
teacher, I thought it was perfect. And it stuck.

I get the names for my boxes based on what happens to
be going on in my life at the time. It can be
anything from wishing that it would snow (hence the
"NY - a Winter Wonderland" series), or having my
wisdom teeth out (The Educated Molars PLB). Sometimes
I use a combination of events and locations. My
"B.U.tterfly" box was done after the Painted Lady
butterflies in my daughter's class hatched, but it's
also located on the Binghamton University nature trail
(B.U.)

Here's wishing everyone a very happy holiday season!
Songchick

From: jmhlittlejohn | Date: 2004-12-23 16:20:02 UTC
Hi Guys,

We have named our boxes a couple different ways. Some I carved first
because I liked the picture (Oak of 1812 and Braddock Duck), some we
planned out thoroughly before carving (NYS Thruway Series), some we
wanted to do just because we liked the area (Little Ruth's), and of
course, the Toxin Series is my "Social Consciousness" contribution.

Some favorite names we came up with are "Still Standing", "Fuchsia
Fish", and "Amanda's Gold". Those ones were fun and
special. "Kelsey's Chicken" I carved for one of our young Onions. I
gave it to her for her birthday and she placed it where she wanted it
to be.

Anyway, the real reason I'm posting is to say
*Merry Christmas* !!
and Happy New Year.

Half-life". Stay tuned.......

Basically, we try to work from a theme (ex--I like sprites, the
Highlander's a duck hunter), but then we go with favorite things or
special areas, too. Most of our Lockport boxes have to do with
Lockport history or my school's group, PACE. It's usually the place
that defines the stamp, but in the case of the gathering, it was the
theme and in the case of Homestar, one of our recent stamps...well,
we just like Homestar! :-)
