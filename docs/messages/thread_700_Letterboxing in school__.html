Letterboxing in school??

From: John & Vicki Tinkler | Date: 2007-10-11 05:38:39 UTC
Hello everyone!  I just thought I'd let you all know about a project I'm trying to get started in my daughter's school. 
Every year our elementary school has a program called PARP (Parents As Reading Partners).  This year I am trying to get the principal to include letterboxing in this event.  I am supposed to meet with her tomorrow.  I thought that it would be a great way to introduce the children to letterboxing in an environment that they are comfortable with. 
 
Here's the idea...each classroom would choose a book to base their clues on.  The other classes would have to read said book to decipher the clues.  Letterboxes would be hidden within the school building and school grounds.  (ex. if a class used "The Very Hungry Catipillar" the letterbox might be hidden in the cafeteria.) 
 
I have spoken to a few teacher's who are all excited about the idea.  Many of them are going to be checking out letterboxing. 
Any ideas?  suggestions?
 
I'll let you know if it all works out, if the school agrees....and also what kind of response we get.
 
 
Take Care,
    ~Vicki (the "Tinklerbelles")

Re: Letterboxing in school??

From: gslbbbanda | Date: 2007-10-11 11:58:00 UTC
I hate to be a party pooper, but on this one, I doubt I'll be the only
one. Please DON'T! For every person that "gets" it, you'll have 5
more that don't (at least!). And the ones that don't ruin it for the
rest of us.

The activity you should try is Questing. It's almost identical to
letterboxing, except that it is based on the idea of teaching people
more about their community.

Check out this website:
www.vitalcommunities.org/ValleyQuest/ValleyQuest.htm

There is an excellent book, "Questing" by Delia Clark and Steven
Glazer that is basically a tutorial on how to set this up. It's one
of the best books I've ever found for creative ideas for clue
writing.

RE: [LbNY] Letterboxing in school??

From: JuneMcAllister | Date: 2007-10-11 23:02:13 UTC
I'm afraid I have to agree with gslbbbanda. Letterboxing is not meant to be a project for a class of little kids. It's hard enough to maintain the specialness and uniqueness of what started out as a wonderful, creative gem of a hobby that spread by word of mouth to the talented and creative individuals who "got it". The huge amount of publicity it has received in articles such as the one in Family magazine bring hoards of people who are turning into a run of the mill, white bread little hobby they think is cute. Please make it a treasure hunt and call it a treasure hunt. Please don't bring more publicity to this hobby than we already have. I know you mean well, but........
 
Missmoon
