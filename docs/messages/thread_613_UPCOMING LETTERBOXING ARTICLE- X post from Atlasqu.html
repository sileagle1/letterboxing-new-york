From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-10-21 02:40:51 UTC
HEADS U. An article about letterboxing is slated to be published in
the Syracuse New Times on Oct. 25th. Yes, I spoke to the writer and
raved about what a wonderful hobby this was..bringing together all
generations and getting people outdoors and exercising, as well as
learning about their local history and other cool topics. I stressed
over and over the "rules" of this hobby..be stealthy while boxing,
leave no trace, and replant the box better than you found it. We
talked about all sorts of things and he also went boxing with me..and
we even discovered one of my boxes muggled...so I think he "gets" it.
Time ( and editing ) will tell. I also told him I didn't care what
else got cut out as long as those three rules made the article.

That being said, I thought it would only be fair to give Central New
York boxers a warning so that they could add F-restrictions, go WOM or
pull their boxes. I know how much some of you NY boxers hate to cater
to newbies that come out of the woodwork after anyone shares this
hobby with others in a public forum.

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-10-22 20:39:24 UTC
This article will be in the Syracuse New Times, a local "alternative" paper like you see in most cities. The reporter told me that he first heard about boxing from one of his friends awhile ago, and then recently he was in an SU class where a local Syracuse boxer explained letterboxing to the class. ( no, it wasn't me). Thus the idea for an article was born. The boxer from his class didn't want to be identified, and his search of other boxers got him a few finders willing to talk but no planters. He finally found me, and I said I would be willing to do it as long as he actually went letterboxing WITH me and as long as it could have a more educational view of letterboxing as the hobby was going through some growing pains in our CNY area.

He DID go boxing with me, and was there to witness me finding one of my boxes muggled and the contents missing. So I reiterated that this is why we like the "rules" of letterboxing stressed in every forum. The photographer naturally wanted to focus on the stamps, and took pictures of MY images only.  The reporter couldn't tell me what would get cut from the article, but promised to make sure that the "rules" I mentioned made it into the writeup. So I am sitting here with bated breath just like the rest of you. And FYI, both my Dome box and my Marshall Street box ( the camera one) were missing when I checked them on Sat. This was BEFORE any article came out.

If the article is butchered, then of course I will be upset. I think I am perfectly capable of WRITING the article for them but of course they wouldn't go for that. So my next best option was to try to get the word out there about muggled boxes and the like by browbeating their young reporter. :)

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-10-24 01:50:50 UTC
Actually, you DID misinterpret me. I am well aware that anti-publicity
and anti-newbie do not go hand in hand. I have been a moderator of
this board since it was LBCNY, I'm a letterboxing blogger and have
been an active poster on Atlasquest for some time...and often talk
about my own personal takes on various letterboxing topics. Because of
this, I get a large volume of mail from every direction (most of it
from the NY area) and some of it is quite heated. I heard from several
NY people that were not happy I agreed to speak to a reporter and
mentioned they would pull their boxes or clues to avoid "catering to
newbies that come out of the woodwork after letterboxing has been
discussed in a public forum." That is a DIRECT QUOTE I got from one of
the angry/concerned boxers, although others said basically the same
thing. But I also believe its perfectly within a boxer's right to
pull his boxes or clues if he feels his boxes might be threatened. I
disagree, but that doesn't mean I don't respect his right to have a
differing opinion. Let me be clear here, I DON'T feel a warning by me
"supplies credence to a non-publicity stance." I warned people as a
COURTESY to those who DO feel a warning is necessary.


I have posted here for over a year, held events where I greeted
and/or helped every newbie, have attended gatherings where I helped
the host(s) greet and/or help every newbie, met privately with new
boxers to escort them around town to some boxes, met with visiting
newbies to bring them to some of my local boxes when they contacted me
(some from as far away as Georgia), put up new boxers in my own home,
gave away hundreds of dollars of lock n locks paid with my own money
just so new ( and old!)people could try them, repaired boxes on the
trail for newbies, helped newbies write their clues, tested directions
for them, etc etc etc. So I am pretty soft when it comes to newbies,
as you can tell! LOL But I am THRILLED to hear that you feel the
same way and are "willing to take the time to talk and explain
politely and patiently to new boxers who are open to learning the
basics and/or arcane aspects of this hobby." I would welcome the help
at the Desperately Seeking Sun gather, if you ( or anyone else) can
make it. I am expecting a LOT of newbies at that gather and they
wouldn't have had much experience boxing before the snow fell. Did I
mention that the County is allowing dogs in the lodge this year? :)

I am sorry if you felt lumped into the "hates catering to newbies."
That was not my intent. But if you truly want, as you say: "this
hobby passed wom from experienced boxer to experienced boxer, keeping
boxes safe from the mishandling, mis-hiding, of over-enthusiastic new
boxers not well-versed in hiding techniques" than I don't see why an
local article will stop you from enjoying the hobby your way. WOM is
your full right!!

Thanks for posting -

LW ( hey, CQ..are you on here yet?)

From: Jen Z | Date: 2006-10-24 16:33:32 UTC
Thanks for clarifying, LockWench. After reading your post I was afraid too, that you thought that those of us who dislike overt publicity about Letterboxing were also inherantly anti-Newbie. I hadn't read anything that strong even with all the debates going on, so I wondered where it was coming from. Not that I can speak for the person who privately emailed you, but I remember reading somewhere (on this board? AQ? The ADK or Oswego LB Boards? Hmm) -- a Boxer was saying that he (she?) disliked published articles because the people who read them and suddenly thought, "Hey, this is a great thing!" all of a sudden start printing clues, going out searching, without researching the ettiquette and "how to's" and then make a mess of the boxes because they hadn't researched the in's and out's of letterboxing properly. Maybe that was what they meant. I can certainly understand that line of thinking-how it would be frustrating, I mean. However, I hope they didn't mean they hate ALL newbies, because we were ALL new boxers once. Heck, I've been boxing for about 2 years and I'm not going to brag--I STILL consider myself a Newbie in several aspects of letterboxing (like compass reading! ARRRRR I just CANNOT figure it out! :oP).

You are very generous to spend so much time out and about teaching new boxers. I have to admit, that's not a strong point of mine. I hate to sound too much like a total recluse, but I'm just uncomfortable around people I really don't know- unless there is a computer screen or phone line between us, LOL. I guess that's what I like most about letterboxing... for me it is a solitary endeavor, just me and my dogs. I don't mind emailing, or answering LB questions that come in from the boxing part of my website (I get them a lot), but face-to-face meeting and greeting is a lot for me to take. LOL, I guess I am definitely more of an animal person than a people person! :o) It is much more comfortable for me to put stuff up on a website than try to explain something face to face, with my stuttering and stammering issues I would make a bad impression anyway. Ah well. We each have our own strengths and weaknesses, I guess. ;o)

Hey...on an unrelated note... I see you are signed up for NaNoWriMo!!!! Are you excited? Do you have your story line all set? 7 days to go!

~Trailhoundz
