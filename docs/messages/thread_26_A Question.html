A Question

From: ducacj | Date: 2004-10-11 17:47:22 UTC
We started letterboxing in May of this year and are loving it.
We've been incorporating it into our weekends and all of our
traveling.
I'm a transplanted NY'er from Rome who now lives in Indiana so we
travel back and forth a few times a year. We have some ideas for
boxes in the central NY area and are hoping to plant at least one in
the beginning of November on our next trip back.
Anyway our question is this. What percentage of quests do you
actually find the letterboxes? On a recent trip to CA we attempted
15 boxes and found 10. It's very frustrating to us to be almost sure
we're in the right place and not find it. I always email the placer
and let them know we attempted it and where we looked exactly. On
only one occasion have we been told we were in the wrong area.
Anyways we were just curious if we're running about average for
attempts vs. finds.
The whole letterboxing experience has shown us some beautiful
areas and taught us some very interesting history.
Cindi

One other thing - how do you know if you find a hitch hiker if it's
not clearly marked. this past week we were in CA letterboxing and
found a known hitch hiker it was clearly labeled and had an email
address to contact. In another box though I found only an extra
stamp it had no writing on it no logbook. It did not seem to match
the stamp or area that it was in. We didn't know if that too is a
hitch hiker and if it was to be moved. Any info on this would be
greatly appreciated. Thanks in advance

From: catbead1 | Date: 2004-10-12 03:08:19 UTC
Hi Cindi,

So far we've been lucky and have only missed one box, our second
attempt. On that one we searched everywhere, behind every single tree
within 60 feet of 'the tree', and were certain the box was gone
finally giving up. We later learned that this box had been MIA for
some time, adopted & replaced and is MIA again (duly noted on the
clues now). Maybe the park people keep finding it....But the day was
nice, we had a lot of fun and wound up with some great photo's.

I think most of the boxes around here (Syracuse) are in place, some
are pulled for the winter but the clues will say that.


HitchHikers are usually marked and come with a logbook. Email the box
owner, maybe they put a bonus stamp in.

Cheers,
catbead

From: Scott and Kathy Trefz | Date: 2004-10-13 00:42:44 UTC
Hi Cindi! I have to say that we have been successful about 95% of the
time. And like you, when we don't locate the box we write to the placer
just to make sure. Most of the time, it is something we didn't do "right" .
I will say, one time we found the tree that the box was planted under and we
froze in our tracks because the trunk and ground all around the tree was
covered in layers and layers of poison ivy. Okay, I don't mind a little
here and there - I usually wear long sleeves and long pants - and I even
have a pair of rubber gloves in my backpack - but WOW, this clump was beyond
my dare! So we were very disappointed not being able to get our hands on
the box - the trail took almost an hour to get to the location!
But, it is all in the joy of letterboxing - that is how we look at it.

There are a lot of NY boxes waiting here for you when you come back for a
visit!!!

Ma of the Poohka clan
