More Stuff from the Road

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-09-24 21:40:31 UTC
Sorry you won't make it down to NYC, Paul. But I do have good news. I
saved a bunch of money on my car insurance. :) No, my good news is
that I checked our Moss Island boxes today and am happy to report
that both boxes are alive and well. We even had visitors from
SPAIN!!! In any case, today's journey was a scouting journey. My
sister and I drove to Stratford to pick up our annual wood kindling
order. You see, we purchase wood used for Louisville Sluggers (which
are made in nearby Dodgeville) and buy the wood that is not good
enough for bats but burns well in my fireplace. After stocking up we
visited two future sites I have chosen for boxes..then drove to
Dodgeville to check out the antique shops in the old Daniel Green
factory complex. Then off to Little Falls to check on the Moss Island
boxes. Profile Rock was busy with over 100 rock climbers today as the
fall rock climbing classes have begun. We made it into the potholes
on Moss Island and checked the boxes (rehiding them as they were both
left out on the shelf in my spot) and then went and grabbed a bite at
Anns'.

Then we headed to my favorite store in the Little Falls Antique and
Gift Center....INKY FINGERS. I bought some new ink pads (those nice
little square ones I carry around in a Lock n Lock) and visited with
the owners. Both of them had been out to Moss Island and found the
boxes with their families..and hooked a BUNCH of people! SO cool. The
owner is so into it that she is now stocking PZ Cut in her store so
if you need some in a hurry...she's got it. She carved her first
stamp and it came out great. I recommend you all go in and check out
her fabulous stamp and ink collections. She has the biggest selection
of unusual stamps that I have ever seen..as well as the Distressed
Inks, the Vintage Inks, the Chalk Inks..the Metallics..you name it.

After that it was a trip to check out two more locations and then a
stop to see my dad in Ilion. We grabbed some tomato pie (yum!) on our
way back to Syracuse and quickly went out to my favorite orchard to
pick apples (Leubner's in Camillus). Had some delicious hot apple
fritters from there for dessert and headed back home with a nice
bushel of Macouns. What a great day....

Lock Wench

What a great day.
