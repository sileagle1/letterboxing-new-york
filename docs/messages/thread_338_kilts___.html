Re: kilts...and other horrifying stuff

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-09-11 13:51:20 UTC
I agree. Price wise, kilts are the male equivalent of a corset. Any
woman who has worn a corset knows the difference between the
cheap "corsets" found at Frederick's and the REAL thing. A kilt, like
a corset, should be something that you save up for and treat
yourself. You will NOT be disappointed...trust me.

As for that link (http://sporrans.com/skunkfulultimate.html), Lisa?
Let's say I am horrified but then again..with some thought...not at
all surprised. Humans have been borrowing the beauty of animals to
supplement their own perceived worthlessness for centuries. At least
they recognize beauty when they see it. The skunk one really is going
a little far though, eh?

Reminds me when I was little. My Roman Catholic father, after much
protest, allowed my Presbyterian mother to start taking our family to
her home church. Our assigned family pew was directly behind this
woman who always proudly wore her real fox cape. This consisted of
many FULL fox pelts sewn together. And when I say FULL...I mean FULL.
Paws, heads..the whole works. The beautiful tails framed her face
which meant the HEADS draped down her back...right at a child's eye
level. Being an animal lover...who felt closer to animals than humans
half the time...you can imagine my horror at being forced to sit
directly behind this woman. I could barely concentrate on prayers,
sermons, hymns or anything, and I remember once bursting into tears
as the minister talked about loving all of God's creatures. My mother
took me by my hand and led me out of services that day and I was
amazed when I arrived the next Sunday to see the woman in a beautiful
maroon velvet coat with elaborate black braid trimming. She smiled at
me and squeezed my hand but never said a word and my mother never
spoke another word about it either. God, I miss my mom.

Lock Wench

From: e_stuckwisch | Date: 2005-09-11 19:12:28 UTC
I was going to reference just that book, but lisascenic beat me to
it! It does drive me nuts to see men wearing "knee socks" with
their kilts, instead of the proper kilt hose. I believe they are
called trews, but I probably spelled that wrong. I haven't got
around to knitting any yet, but I beleive I have a pattern in one of
my other sock books!

On a different kilt note, I was at a wedding with a bagpiper and was
glad that he chose an undergarment, although not thrilled it was a
pair of white y-fronts. Men don't tend to know how to pick stuff up
off the ground when wearing a "skirt." I was right behind him!
Those things can be short, especially if the guy is a little round!

And I do agree with Lock Wench and lisascenic that a kilt is
something to save up for. You definately want it to be well made
from the best materials (i.e. wool: don't even talk to me about
acrylic kilts!) and as a fiber artist I can tell you that that does
cost. If your plaid is harder to get, you may even be paying a
cottage industry weaving studio. Skilled labor is an expensive,
dying art. Sometimes I think the only folks still getting by on it
are a few carpenters and autobody workers.

knitica

p.s. I speak for Rusty when I suggest that real dead bunny on the
sporin is not the same sort of luxury as real wool. There are some
really nice fake furs out there!
