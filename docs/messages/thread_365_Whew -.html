month to do maintenance on all the boxes I have out there. Or get

hear from me for a month after that...it's because I'm on sabbatical!

Georgia,
I believe you can find answers to all of the above questions on the
very useful site, www.atlasquest.com

If you haven't checked it out yet, you should take a look, and join
(small fee, but worth it, and there's a free version, too)
Atlasquest not only has tutorials, FAQs, and message boards, but
probably the most useful part is that you can keep an online log of
all your finds and plants -- and it notifies you when someone finds
your box.

Also special searches for areas, and for trips, so you can letterbox
along the way. You can also host clues for your letterboxes on this
site.

It connects to letterboxing.org, but adds on many functions and info
that is on the LbNA site. Plus, longtime letterboxer and atlasquest
web site developer, Ryan, makes continual improvements. It just
keeps getting better.

Paul in SF

Re: Whew -

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-10-11 17:27:24 UTC
"I don't get this whole foam thing. I know what you do with it, but I
don't see the point. I've never used foam and my images come out just
fine. I've never encountered another stamp with a foam backing and
they all came out ok."

I think Norasta is saying that she has found a medium that is fast,
easy and inexpensive..and still leaves a nice image. I know many
stamp making classes use foam for beginners because it is so easy to
work with.

As for BACKING stamps in foam...I love it! Some of my stamps are a
little bigger and therefore unstable and the foam backing really
helps leave a nice, complete image. Some of the stamps I find are
very tiny...and the foam backing the carver has used makes them MUCH
easier to use! I find I stay CLEANER stamping in with foam-backed
stamps as well. My finger tips stay further away from the ink. I
only recently started backing MY stamps with foam and it was really
easy and much appreciated by others.
