Being someone who frequents Derby Hill at all times of the year,
I'll give you my take on the dog situation. Yes, they say dogs are
allowed as long as they behave. But....during the times of the year
when the raptor migration is in full swing, I wouldn't recommend it,
even though they allow it. Here's why: In the early spring months
that place is packed- I mean PACKED- with bird-watching folks. They
come in droves! They set up camp for the whole weekend and don't
budge! That parking area at the top of the hill will be completely
full! These are hardcore birdwatchers, and they're the kind of
people who might have a a problem with dogs. I just don't want to
risk them getting miffed at us letterboxers and start hurling boxes
into the lake.

This time of year, if you want to get the Derby Hill boxes, you're
perfectly fine if you want to take the dogs (by the way Trailhoundz,
we're DYING TO MEET THEM!!!!) because it's deserted most of the
time. It's just the early spring months that I would avoid with the
dogs.

MTNestRobin took her dog and I know they had no problem.
