From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-09-25 02:46:49 UTC
Yes, I was once an obsessive knitter, too -- it was how I coped in a very
difficult period of time (Remember Nepenthe?). Then I went into obsessive
quilting for art wall quilts. Now I'm just plain obsessed. At the moment,
I'm obsessed with completing the sound editing & mixing for a piece of music
I composed, titled 'Fugue in A', where the fugue is of the mental flight of
fantasy kind... With over 34 tracks, it's fairly complicated, but far less
manic than knitting ;-) I'm self-publishing it on CD in limited quantities,
with original cover art.

How about this: I do an afternoon in Oakland with you, and you do an
afternoon in SF with me? Oakland is like a foreign country to me -- why
don't people in SF cross the Bay Bridge to go there? What little I've seen
is either lovely (Lake Merritt, Berkeley campus) or interesting (Jack London
Square, Berkeley campus). Oh yes, I took a class in glass at the Crucible in
West Oakland once, too. I want to go to Oakland's Art Museum and try to find
Princess Lea's letterboxes there... and maybe smell some roses. BTW, the
Dahlia Garden at the Conservatory of Flowers is in full riotous color right
now (I think there are 3 boxes near there).

We can visit and picture our NY comrades bundling up for a long, freezing
cold winter... being bicoastal does have its advantages...

I have 3 more free weeks away from work... will you be done perling by then?

Paul
