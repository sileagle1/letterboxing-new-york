From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-01-25 16:07:10 UTC
My favorite was in creating the underwater letterbox that I mentioned here
before. It was called, "The Hunt for the Temple of Neptune in the former
city of Atlantis, Tlamco".

There's been a legend here in San Francisco that goes back to the late 1800s
that says that SF was once the site of the city Tlamco, in Atlantis. (And
this was well before the LSD days!). Psychics say that they've identified
the sites of the former locations of several of the temples once located
here. They are arranged much like the paths of the planets they are named
after, with the Temple of the Sun at the center, appropriately sitting smack
dab in Haight-Ashbury.

The Temple of Neptune supposedly sat on Strawberry Hill which is a small
island on Stow Lake in Golden Gate Park. This is a interesting location, as
a manmade waterfall runs down Strawberry Hill, and there's a colorful
Chinese pavilion at its base. Three small wooden bridge pass back & forth
over the waterfall on a path leading to the top of the hill. So the
letterbox was in a pool of water on a ledge in the waterfall, attached to
the bridge by monofilament line, and kept in place in the water with a lead
weight.

To find the box, you had to read a map, unscramble an inscription anagram,
and head to Stow Lake. Then you had to find two small boxes hidden on
Strawberry Hill first. I gave 5 possible locations, so 3 were false leads.
When you found the 2 boxes, inside each was 1/2 of a cryptic diagram, and
some strange writing (Atlantean code). Also inside each were 2 strips of
flexible polymer clay, that looked like old metal. One had strange
characters, the other, English letters. By lining up beads on them, you
could decode the word, "bridge", and the diagram suddenly made sense,
because it showed a line tied to a bridge and going down to the water. All
that was left was to check the three bridges.

This letterbox was wonderfully and diabolically complex. I had to create a
whole story about the Temple, and figure out how to make an underwater box
work. The carving was really nice -- a woman wearing a tunic, walking, and
blowing a large, long trumpet.

FUN!!!

From: songchick | Date: 2005-01-25 23:00:17 UTC
OK - ours revolves around the Letchworth Park box in
WNY ...

Every year a group of friends and my family go camping
for a long weekend, somewhere in NY State. We had
chosen Letchworth that year, as we had never been
there and wanted to check out the gorges.

I had "recruited" a bunch of my buddies to go
letterboxing with. So we start out with 6 adults, 2
kids under 5, and a terrier down into the gorge.
There was a forecast for scattered T-storms, but we
figured we could make it. About 10 minutes into the
hike two of the adults decided it was getting to dark
and cloudy, and took their son back with them to our
campground. But the rest of us (including the 5 year
old and the dog) continued on.

About halfway to the box, the deluge began. I can
honestly say that I would have been drier standing
under one of the waterfalls. We got to the site, and
what was described in the clues as a "trickling
stream" looked more like the Mississippi. But we
jumped over at the narrowest spot, built a tent of
raincoats, and stamped in.

After the box was safely planted, we were going to
head back when one of the girls dropped her water
bottle into the "stream". Like an idiot I dashed into
the stream, water up to my thighs, and retrieved the
bottles just before the went under a chain link fence
and down what looked like a 50' drop. Needless to say
I backed out of there pretty darn quick.

By the time we made it back to the campground, it had
stopped raining and we were all laughing hysterically.
And believe it or not, we're all still
letterboxing!!!
