LBWNY or LBNY (crosspost)

From: Daina | Date: 2005-08-17 15:44:41 UTC
So, as long as we're still waiting for shirts, it occured to me that
our shirts might be more friendly if we include the rest of the state.
After all our Central NY neighbors and downstate brethern join us for
our events quite often (and vice versa) and they plant quite a bit
themselves (hence, the universality of "If You Plant It, They Will
Come"!). Would anyone have any objection to taking out the W in LBWNY
and just making the shirt say Letterboxing New York?

Or, those of you who don't live in the WNY area...would you be more
interested in the shirts if we made a change like that? We're not
trying to sell more shirts or anything (since we're not getting any
money ourselves for this) but we suddenly felt snobby for making it
Western New York only.

Let's hear some discussion on this...we've got about a week before
final changes can be made, so...?

The Sprite & the Highlander

Re: [LbCNY] LBWNY or LBNY (crosspost)

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2005-08-17 18:24:58 UTC


 Would anyone have any objection to taking out the W in LBWNY and just making the shirt say Letterboxing New York?

Funny that you should mention this....as the LBCNY group had a meeting this summer and discussed how best to include all New Yorkers in NY letterboxing activities. When Paul in SF started the LBCNY group over a year ago, it was because NY State had an identity crisis. Some New Yorkers felt out of place in LB Mid Atlantic group....but not quite at home in the LB Northeast. His intent was not to split NY State into separate areas, but to "centralize"  NYS letterboxing so that ALL New Yorkers could/would participate. Let's face it...we are WAYYY bigger than the tiny state of Connecticut with millions of acres of undeveloped forests,lakes,waterfalls and trails and counting NYC we have a millions of potential letterboxers. Sounds like NY State could easily stand on it's own. To be perfectly honest..and speaking ONLY for myself..and this is in NO WAY a slight to all the hard work that Sprite has done..... I was little dismayed to see the start of formal regional groups like LBWNY or others surely soon to follow, but maybe this is just me. I would STILL attend an LBWNY group function or a Catskills group one or an Adirondack group function or a Finger Lakes function,or...well....you get the picture.

I like the idea of there being ONE group for all of NY State, as I believe there is better identity and strength in numbers AND strength in pooled dollars.  This may seem like sacrilege to the original members of LBCNY and I would NEVER intentionally insult any of them OR their hard work. It just seems to me that it would be more "I Love New York-ish" to include everyone in one big happy group, and enjoy regional gatherings such as the Finger Lakes, the Western Gathering, the Fear Faketor or whatever.

That being said..I am not really a T shirt wearer so my vote doesn't count on the shirt change. But I gotta say I would be more enticed to buy them as gifts if it was a shirt that encompassed all of NY State. :)

Just my two cents -

Lock Wench

Re: LBWNY (now The United State of NY!)

From: Daina | Date: 2005-08-17 23:05:49 UTC
Thanks for the compliment and the thought provoking comments (see, I
knew when I posted here, I'd get genuine feedback...love ya,
Lockie!). I thought I better explain why we made the other group to
make sure everyone understood our motives...I'd hate for folks to
think we started a union rift. When we first chose to do a separate
group for Yahoo, it was for three reasons:

1) Our Meetup group, where we were keeping a list of folks that would
attend our gathering, turned into a pay site, which we DID NOT want
to do. We wanted to have a central place to post gatherings for the
area (this was before AQ had an event posting spot, of course)

2) This group was just Central New York, which led us to believe that
there were SUPPOSED to be separate groups (like a downstate group and
ourselves). We never even considered saying LBNY because it seemed
too all-encompassing for the whole state (and a little pretentious
with there already being a CNY). I don't think Paul in SF had
considered saying that either, so I'm guessing he had the same idea.

3) Even though we've all been welcome in both groups (and have
attended gatherings in all regions), it's still relatively hard for
those of us in Buffalo/Niagara (and even Rochester) to join Syracuse
folks for boxes & events and vice versa. Out of 54 people, only
Crafymouse, Dandelion, MTNestRobin & MTNMan & Missmoon came from CNY
& downstate to our first WNY event and I think only RocNRon and
ourselves came from WNY to the Finger Lakes event. I think we and the
Quinns were the ONLY ones from WNY at Fear Faketor, so it's even more
of a challenge to head out to the Catskills and NYS way.

We really liked how CNY folks were able to connect and get together
for boxing on the weekends, but it was out of the question for most
of us to join you. So, having a group where someone could say "Hey,
we're boxing in Willamsville...anyone want to come?" and knowing we
could actually GO was cool. It's only recently that we've seen more
folks from other areas come up to box in our neck of the woods.

So, I wanted to be sure no one thought we did this to cause a rift in
NYS. In fact, I'd really RATHER have a whole state board and if the
shirts are part of that, I certainly don't mind (which is why I was
asking). As of late, it seems like planning events in Rochester makes
it an equal haul for everyone, so we've got a balance again. And
regardless, I don't think anyone ever considered NOT going to an
event because of the region's name (I know WE haven't!)

As far as Yahoo groups go, I would love to merge into one solid LBNY
group, but again, I'm not sure that's something everyone would want.
Most people have a link to both groups (less with WNY, of course),
but again, all of us under Central New York makes it more of a
Syracuse/Ithaca thing than a full state thing, just like WNY suggests
Buffalo/Rochester. It's good to know that some folks are thinking
about it (especially enough to have a meeting), but even that meeting
makes it difficult to imaging us bridging the distance gap. We'dve
love to have been part of that, if we could have.

So, regardless, I'm changing the shirt, but I think we've got a whole
other topic to consider. How can we unite under one common roof
without becoming this side and still accomodate the distance between
us all?

From: songchick | Date: 2005-08-18 02:49:52 UTC
This is coming from someone who technically doesn't
live in either CNY or WNY ... Binghamton is the
"Southern Tier" along with Elmira and other places
along the NY/PA border, so here's my $.02

I joined both groups so that I could cover all of
upstate NY. Syracuse is about 1 1/2 hours drive for
us, Ithaca is just under an hour. Rochester and
Buffalo are considerably farther, but we're up there
frequently to visit friends so I wanted to get in
touch with boxers from that area as well.

NY is a HUGE state, but not impossible to join
together. I'd love to see the groups combine into a
big NY boxing group. My personal preference is the
upstate boxes ... it's just too hard for me to get
down to NYC and LI, and I have a HUGE aversion to
traffic jams (chuckle), but maybe if I got to know
some of the boxers from that area of NY, I'd feel
differently.

The bottom line is, we're all interested in the same
thing ... filling up this beautiful state of ours with
as many letterboxes as we can cram into trees and
under rocks. And that's what really matters.

Looking SO forward to seeing everyone on the 27th ...
we'll be a bit late since we're going to the Herp show
in Syracuse that morning, but leaving in plenty of
time to get to the gathering.

Songchick
