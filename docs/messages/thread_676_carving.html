When carving with the razor knife, you always want to hold the blade
at an angle, so the rubber remaining is narrowest at the inking
surface and gets broader below. And you must constantly keep in mind
which way is which, otherwise you may undercut some of the rubber
inking surface. You'll eventually need to learn to cut with the
blade moving either towards you or away from you.

At first, make as shallow a cut as you can make, just barely
scratching the surface of the rubber with the very point of the
blade. Go over the entire pattern that way before making any deep
cuts or removing any rubber at all. While cutting, remember that the
location of the blade is less important than the location of the edge
of the blade. In fact, you can often cut tiny zig-zags without
lifting the knife; just twist, and it'll push the rubber out of the
way as it cuts sideways.

For all of you magnificent carvers out there, could someone please
explain this to me in English. for example, what is undercutting? Do
you hold the blade at an angle with the blade on the line you want to
cut and the handle lower towards you? When talking about the rubber
being narrowest at the inking surface and broader below, what exactly
does that mean? I have tried to carve 6 stamps. Three with an Xacto
and 3 with a speedball and none of them look like the image. I have
tried both negative and positive carving. They are all awful. I

From: JuneMcAllister | Date: 2007-08-06 22:41:07 UTC
Personally I get nothing from complicated directions like that. I learned
by doing, trial and error.
Did you trace the image, then turn it over and rub it onto the carving
medium? I rub it on with the edge of a coin and that seems to work very
well for me. If you are doing that it should look like the image. Then you
have to decide what part of it looks best positive or negative. I also find
that I get a MUCH better and clearer image rubbed on if I carve on PZ cut
grade B. Sometimes it's easier to move the piece of carving stuff rather
than move the carving tool.

I used to use the pink stuff until Mizscarlet turned me onto PZ Cut Grade
B. Now I won't carve on anything else. I don't like Mastercarve and some
swear by it. I do all of my carving with the smallest speedball tool,
except for large areas I want to get rid of, so I can't help with any Xacto
advice. I prefer positive carving. Are you using a magnifying light. Makes
a :::::HUGE:::: difference, at least for me. You can get a lamp that goes
on the side of a desk or table or magnifying glasses you wear on your head
like a miner's thingie. I prefer the tabel model. You'll get tons of
different advice and methods from different people. You have to find what
works best for you.
