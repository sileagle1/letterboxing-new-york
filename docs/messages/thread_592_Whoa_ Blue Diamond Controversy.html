Whoa! Blue Diamond Controversy

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-09-25 12:22:57 UTC
I knew when I woke Sunday morning and was one of the first to express
a strong opinion regarding the new Blue Diamond rating system, that I
was potentially making myself a target. That's the risk you take in
this country when you voice an opinion. But man oh man! LOL

While I am happy to report that the AQ and regional list communities
kept their comments pretty much non-personal, you wouldn't believe the
volume of private email I am getting. *grin* But people are doing
exactly what I asked. I asked for people to write to me and share
their views on this new feature, as I was struggling to get my mind
wrapped around it..as I initially saw so many cons...and very few pros.

Thanks to all who wrote...even those who were nasty. I especially like
the person who told me I needed to attend a Weight Watchers meeting or
two. I am not sure what that has to do with Blue Diamond ratings, but
obviously I am out of the running for their own personal rating
system. Imagine my disappointment. :)

But back to the pros and cons. I liked the fact that people brought up
some good points on BOTH sides of the issue. Things I didn't see at
first. Personally, I still am happy with my decision to opt out. But I
don't have any problem with people who decide to use the system. I am
a little sad, thinking that I perhaps won't get nice notes about a
visit to one of my boxes anymore...and may just get a rating that I
will never see anyway. But that's the choice I made when I opted out.
I can live with it.

In deference to those who may not get a chance to toot their own horn,
or who are so new (or so experienced) that their boxes have not
received any designation, I would like to ask our community here.
Which box have you planted that YOU are personally the most proud of?
Doesn't have to mean a great stamp or a location, but it could. Just
tell us which is your alltime fave of all your plants and why. Planted
letterboxes are a labor of love for the majority of us, and I would
love to hear your stories. Maybe others would too.

LW

From: Tracy Baxter Haynes | Date: 2006-09-25 16:58:17 UTC
Never ceases to amaze me how nasty people can be.  It looks to be like it is simulating what passes for national discourse in our country these days.  I, for one, believe that people can discuss, have different points of view and still be polite and respectful.  Hey, like I always told my kids, 'Different is good" :-)  It is always a shame when people can't articulate their positions and defend them without resorting to the childish and personal. 
 
As far as the Blue Diamond system goes, it is just another bell and whistle that doesn't appeal to my Luddite soul :-)  Next time I venture over to AQ, I will probably disable the feature on my boxes, if I can figure out how to do so :-).  I know that some love all the little features and (what I think of as) fou-fous that make up AQ, but I find myself using that site less and less.  I would even venture to say that if LBNA ever adopts a mileage-focused locator like AQ, my visits to AQ might drop like a precipice.  Not because I don't think the site is great, because I think by any measure, AQ is a phenomenal site and quite a marvel in programming, but it's bells and whistles fit in less and less as to how I 'play the game' lately.
 
My favorite box(es) planted would have to be The Seven Wonders.  It is a long hike (almost 12 miles, which I like), in a beautiful location,  but because it is on jeep trails, it is drivable by car, atvs or bikes.  This series takes you by an old cemetery with many early graves and several lakes, one with a cool 'causeway' that you hike across to retrieve one of the boxes.  What makes these my favorites though are the stamps.  The carvings are simply magnificent.  The 8 stamps were carved by Budgie, Seekher, Spudhund, Trailhoundz and myself.  The three I carved, of course, are the lesser and more amateur ones :-/.  From the comments I have received about these stamps, it is clear that the finders appreciate the quality and the care that went into the design and carving of them.
 
~artichoke

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-09-25 22:44:36 UTC
LOL  You guys are too much :)  But don't worry about me receiving nastiness. I DID ask for people to contact me privately with their opinions. And I DID put myself into the arena. Luckily, I still have all that "thick skin" that my "admirer" mentioned...so I should be good to go. *grin*

I just want to let y'all know that I won't be rating your boxes. No offense, but obviously the system is not going away, and obviously it is not accurate yet and Ryan has no way of predicting when it would be. I would rather just write a personal note to each of you and let you know how I enjoyed the outing/box/stamp/location, whatever... as I don't have enough finds to make my opinion carry any real weight in the scheme of things. I am more of a "One Man, One Vote, All Votes Count the Same"  kind of girl.I  will continue to make personal recommendations of boxes to visitors, however...as I think THAT system was working just fine and didn't need any help. You should also know that numbers of hits on your clue pages gives you a better chance of earning a diamond, if that is something that interests you. Having every person you know rank your box high will help your chances too. Am I advocating this? Heck no.

I am just glad I opted out for a lot of reasons.  The thought of some poor sap  from out of town with one hour to spend going to ONE of my previously listed "Blue Diamonds" made me sick to my stomach. It got a lot of hits simply because people were curious about the name and several people  mentioned it in a post.   And my truly best efforts got nary a mention. A system is only good if people can put their trust in it....and I, for one, cannot. Nor am I content with people having to depend on such a system for recommendations until enough people actually find my boxes and report back. I do not live in a letterboxing mecca, and it could take YEARS to get enough votes to make a difference. No thanks.

But that's not even the real problem. The real problem is that once again, newbies are given the short end of the stick. If they don't have enough finds, then they are considered too inexperienced to judge a fantastic box when they see one and so their vote doesn't count as much.... while people who have TONS of finds are allowed to impart their "wisdom" on us once again. No thanks.

As for missing/retired boxes? Just take comfort in the fact that enough people still enjoyed it to rank it highly when it was around..or give it a lot of clue hits for whatever reason. If someone with a lot of finds found it and voted..that person's opinion helped you even more.

LW
