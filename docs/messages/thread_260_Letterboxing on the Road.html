Letterboxing on the Road

From: Lisa Lazar | Date: 2005-08-07 14:07:19 UTC
So, my sweetie Robb and I are in the midst of a cross country drive,
and have been letterboxing along the way. Well, sort of. This is a
big, mostly empty country, after all.

I wanted to share an image from the site of "A Corny Letterbox" which
is on the border between Nebraska and Wyoming. Lovely little stamp!
We saw an astonishing variety of wildflowers at this stop, as well as
wild bunnies and a peregrine falcon. But this sign, which is
mentioned in the clues, really got our attention.

I've posted the link to:
http://photos.groups.yahoo.com/group/lbcny/lst?.dir=/&.src=gr&.view=t
Just click away!

Cheers!
Lisa

Re: Letterboxing on the Road

From: Lisa Lazar | Date: 2005-08-07 15:18:39 UTC
No sticks in this arid landscape, but Robb says "I did poke into the
hole I reached into with somethin' or other." I was wearing flip
flops (my previous poison ivy is still on the mend, and I want to keep
shoes off as much as possible) and spent a lot of time looking at the
ground around my toes. Instead of rattlesnakes, I found crappy
fossils, and some lovely quartz crystals.

We planted a box along the way, but carving at 80 miles per hour seems
a bit risky....

Lisascenic
