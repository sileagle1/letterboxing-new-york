Greeting everyone! I am new to letterboxing in this area. I moved
here a couple of years ago (after a divorce) and just started to get
into boxing around here. I truly enjoy the people, places, and
locations in the Mexico, NY area. I have worked with the Derby Hill
Bird Observatory almost from the day I moved to NY. Birds are my
peace. We work VERY VERY hard as to NOT upset any of our feathered
friends, and although dogs are great (I own two), you need to be
careful in bringing them here. Our mission @ DHBO is:
To contribute to the preservation of raptor species which migrate
through Northeastern America.
The collection of data each spring on the numbers of raptors (hawks)
migrating past Derby Hill using guidelines established by the USFW.
Provision of information and education to the general public about
raptors, other species of birds and the importance of habitat
preservation.
Provision of Derby Hill Bird Observatory as a site for amateurs and
professionals to study and enjoy raptor migration.

*****As for CONTACTING people within the organization about
letterboxes, DO NOT DO IT!! Do not mention the hiding of unnatural
items within the boundries. YES, we want people to visit it. In
the Spring, you can't find a spot, but be sure, we, as an org.,
GREATLY frown upon folks snooping around and being destructive,
while looking for one of my great joys, letterboxes. Use common
sense. Don't be THAT person. Enjoy DHBO. Go find all those
wonderful boxes over there. Have a great time, but be careful about
how you do it, and to whom you talk to about it.

With all that being said, and I sound kind of like a jerk (I'm not.
Just passionate), I look forward to seeing you all on the trail or
at DH. Your friend in letterboxes, lbcnyer
