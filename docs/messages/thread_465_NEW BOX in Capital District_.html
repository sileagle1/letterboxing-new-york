In celebration of the new ADK talk list and to give letterboxing dancers a chance for a winter box, here's a box planted yesterday for your enjoyment. The box is in a sheltered area and should be accessible all year long.

DANCE FLURRY LETTERBOX
Planted by Mookie Fish
Plated on Feb. 11, 2006
County: Saratoga
Nearest city: Saratoga Springs


During the third week in February 2006
they will be tapping & swinging and doing high kicks.
Whether you hip-hop, honky-tonk or belly dance,
swing, salsa, tango or just shake your pants,
the annual Dance Flurry Festival will chase away the blues -
just remember to wear your soft-soled shoes.

To find this special event-themed treasure,
head to Saratoga Springs for business or pleasure.
Begin at the bronze ballet slippers left behind on a stair.
In front of Putnam Market you will find the pair.
From there, head north and find a large clock.
It is on a corner at a bank just up the block.
Head in the direction of noon and cross the street,
and listen to the tip-tap of your dancing feet.

At the Bonacio sign, follow the walk to the fish.
Warm up your hips with a back-and-forth swish.
Next door, find the old movie house ticket booth
where older Saratogians saw shows in their youth.
With your back to the booth, look for a horse.
If dancing is your thing, this place is the source.
This building is headquarters for this dance fest.
Now cross the street to the horses and take a rest.

Choose the middle set of steps and look around
When the coast is clear, look for a gap near the ground.
It is straight ahead in the middle of the horses three
A huge, round, white planter with a space below snug and tiny.
Look in there and then do a little jig,
because the hand-carved stamp is really something to dig!
