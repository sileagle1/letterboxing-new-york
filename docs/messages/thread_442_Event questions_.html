From: Jen Z | Date: 2006-01-11 17:18:40 UTC
Hi everyone,
 
I have some questions about the event coming up in February, so I thought I would ask...
 
1. Will nametags be provided? My greyhounds will not be attending (I don't think the Skyview Lodge allows them in, plus Lucy would probably kill me in my sleep for bringing her on a 5 hour "hike" in the cold, ROFL!). HOWEVER, since they will not be attending, I am- (*corny alert! corney alert!*) making an iron-on TShirt that has their photo on it, as well as our team name- TRAILHOUNDZ- across it. So people will be able to identify me (and hopefully ask about my newly-posted personal traveller? LOL!). But I was wondering how I would be able to identify other people for exchanges, personal travelers, etc! Will people wear nametags?
 
2. My other question- would it be possible for clues to be posted a few days before the event? So we can plan out what boxes are along what trails, what boxes to do in what order, etc.? I remember the Rochester event clues being posted a week or so beforehand, because I was able to print out a trail map, decide which boxes were on what trails and which to go to first, etc. Of course, then Lucy ran full-speed into the camper and I spent the day at the vet's office and not at the gathering... but it did make for nice pre-planning! :o)
 
Well, those are my two questions. I can't wait for my FIRST event! :o)

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-01-11 18:58:38 UTC
Yes,I will definately be handing out name tags. I can't keep 50+ people straight! It will help when I take pictures too. Of course, they will be the stick on type so we will see how long they hold up.
 
As for clues before hand, I really don't want to post the clues before the event...although I MIGHT post them the night before. I am doing this slightly differently....in that I am using the Highland Forest's own trail map as part of my clues..I might say, Look for the Horse Corral, then do ----- and such. You would find the Horse Corral on the map and go to it, then figure out the rest of the clues. Several of the boxes will not be set up until that day,  as they will be inside boxes or "special" boxes. You will understand what "special" means when you get there. When you check in, you will be given a packet including the trail map, a clue booklet ( including personal travelers of those attending) and a BINGO card. I basically want everyone to start on the same page.
 
I don't think Sprite posted her clues in advance of our gathering. I may be wrong, but I don't think so. I remember checking in periodically to see if the clues were up so I could locate my own donated box, but I don't think they were up too early. The boxes for the event ( other than the inside & special ones) will be kept there for 30 days, to give people a chance to get them all, if they wish. Then a couple will be planted permanently and the rest returned or moved to other locations. I don't want to leave too many boxes there as I might decide to do another event there at some point and don't want to overrun the entire park!
 
On a side note, the Winterfest will also be taking place that weekend, and as you know, there is a medallion hunt in County Parks as part of those festivities. I am a little worried about my boxes being muggled, but I have made stickers for all of them saying: "Please leave me here. I am NOT part of the Medallion Hunt!"   Sigh. This should be interesting.
 
Lock Wench

From: Daina | Date: 2006-01-12 01:13:01 UTC
I don't post a full week before our WNY gathering, but I actually do
post them ahead of time, usually that Wednesday or Thursday (or in
one extreme case, I e-mailed them to someone who was coming down
early and leaving early). It's for the out of towners to get their
clues in order while planning out their trip (in fact, as out of
towners for your gathering, we'll be needing some of those early
clues ourselves!). ;-)

I usually mention which ones will be permanent or not, too, just so
visitors can get the boxes that'll be gone first.

Hope that helps!

From: Cyndie | Date: 2006-01-13 22:39:51 UTC
Our local newspaper hides a medallion in one of our parks...and each
day posts a new clue in the paper. If you find it, it is worth $1,000
and if you are a subscriber, it is worth $2,000. I actually called
the papers and told them I was having a scavenger hunt in Highland
Forest and asked if that would be a problem. They didn't tell me if
the medallion was hidden there but said our event was only on the
second day of clues and there wasn't much "action" that early. You
see, the clues get more and more specific as the hunt goes on.

As for snow? I can't help you there, but Highland Forest is just as
fun for a hiker as a skier/snowshoer. :)
