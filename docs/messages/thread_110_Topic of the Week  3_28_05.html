Can you say "Cabin Fever"??? I've tried twice already to go out and
do checks and scout locations. There's been enough snow or mud to
make things impossible.

On a related note...
I've spent my spare time designing a series to plant along
Chautauqua's Rails to Trails. I have permission from the Trail
Manager to plant boxes. It's going to be a train, each box is a
railway station with a different railroad car in it. First person to
correctly identify which car is in which station will get a first
finder's certificate.

The task will involve not only hiking the trails, but driving around
trying to find the starting points for each of the trails (Some of
them aren't as obvious as they should be) as well as solving my clues.
By my estimate, it would take approx. 10 hours to track down every
box in the series (figuring on at least 25 miles of driving, and about
10 miles of walking). My question, knowing this in advance, are
people more likely to plan an early start of it and try to get the
entire series done in one day, or find a few boxes each time and
reserve space in their albums for future finds in the series?

-- Dagonell the Pirate
P.S. I've planted individual boxes before, but this is my first
attempt at series which is why I'm asking questions. If I'm going to
put this much time in it, I'd like to do it right!
-- D
