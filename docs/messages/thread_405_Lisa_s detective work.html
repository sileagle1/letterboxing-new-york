I figured you weren't being mobbed by a bluebird. They are sweet
little birdies. Jays are cheeky brats, and I love them both, with
different parts of my heart.

I remember when I saw my first Eastern Blue Bird, I was driving to do
some decorative painting work in a very posh suburb of DC, and I
almost drove my car off the road, because I couldn't believe what I
was seeing. I was so excited, that I was babbling to all the other
contractors on the job, who all seemd really unimpressed. I
enventually figured out that they thought I had seen a Blue Jay, which
is nice, but no big deal.

New York state has done a great job in bringing back the Eastern Blue
Bird. These birds nest in holes in trees, and are very social. So
you'll see a lot of yellow (why??) houses in pairs in fields all over
rural New York.

I'm such a big softy (and I'm often a little sleep deprived during my
summmers at the Opera) that I almost burst into tears at one Fourth of
July parade, where the Cub Scout had a float about the three hundred
bird boxes they had created. They built a giant bird box, and dressed
someone's little sister as a bluebird, who was holding a sign saying
"thank you for all our houses." People who see my bad ole post-punk
self are often amused that I'm such a nature lover....
