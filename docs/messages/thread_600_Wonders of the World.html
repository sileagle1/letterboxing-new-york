Wonders of the World

From: Pamela Smith | Date: 2006-10-08 22:26:48 UTC
First off, THANKS to everyone who had a hand in planning this. Miss
Buggy and I had a whole lot of fun. The weather was just perfect, the
trees were starting to change colors, I finally met a lot of people I
knew only online and saw a few old friends, and the hunting was
great. We didn't quite make it through all o fthe boxes out on the
trail, but we tried. Oh, and Miss Buggy enjoyed the creek. :-) Most
of the carvings put mine to shame - it's wonderful to see such
creations. Maybe someday I'll be able to pull something like that
off. [I forgot to write down the name of the carver, but 'I speak for
the trees...' was absolutely stunning. Wow!!!]

Two quick things:

1. I think I left a dark blue windbreaker somewhere. If anyone found
one, please let me know.

2. I have one unidentified stamp image..... it's small, less than an
inch long, and looks kind of like a porcupine. If anyone can help me
identify the name of the stamp and the carver, I'd appreciate it.

What a wonderful day. Thanks again to the organizers.
-pezpam

From: LetHerBox@aol.com | Date: 2006-10-08 23:02:11 UTC
Thank you to all the planners of Wonders of the World. It was my first gathering and I was so impressed with the quality and thoughtfulness of everything. 

I want to apologize, though, to the After World gathering folks: I left the event with the intention of finding a few boxes and then going to the dinner. When I almost missed a stop sign, I realized I was pretty fried from a full day of 'boxing, cooties (whoever put that one on my sunglasses made me laugh out loud when I found it), personal travelers, exchanges, trail stamps, etc. I had never even met another boxer before today so it was a lot to keep in my head. So I decided I should get myself home before I got even more tired. I'm sorry to not have said good-bye!

That said, I am looking forward to meeting many of you again. Tamsyn — email me for a trail date and I will give you my phone #!!

Happy trails,
Mookie
