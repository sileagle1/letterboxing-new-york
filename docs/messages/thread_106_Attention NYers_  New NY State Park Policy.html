Dear Libby,
 
I want to share with you something I read in the geocaching forum the other day that may affect us letterboxers. I'm not a geocacher, but recently got a GPS and want to do some benchmarking so I was wandering through their site when I came across this thread started on Jan31and ending a few days ago.
 
NY Park and Rec Survey    http://forums.groundspeak.com/GC/index.php?showtopic=89985  
 
There is a lot to read if you have the time, but the gist is this, NY State Parks and Recreation has implemented a new policy regarding geocaches placed in NYS Parks. They are requiring geocachers to apply for a permit to obtain stickers that must be placed on the cache itself. There is no charge for the permit , however there are restrictions. I've attached the Terms & Conditions and a pdf of the Permit Application.
 
 This has a lot of implications for letterboxing in NY. I fear we are next! In fact, near the end of the aforementioned  thread someone actually did write "What about letterboxing?" but it was quietly overlooked except for a small reference in  a reply.
 
 I don't know if the "Powers that Matter" know about us yet, but it won't be long before some geocacher applying for a permit says, "How come the Letterboxers don't have to comply?" and it will be in our laps! I know you have a lot of letterboxes in Green Lakes SP and since one of the restrictions is no more than five caches, per park, per person  its going to affect you personally if washes over onto us (letterboxers).
 
We have a window of opportunity to act, but how I'm not sure! I haven't sorted out my own thoughts on this but time's awasting and I thought others need to know about it!  I also thought you would have a better idea of what to do with this information than I. With your letterbox contacts maybe you could bounce it around for ideas first. Should we play it close to our chest or have it go out on a list... I don't know! I trust you to have a more rational perspective than I.
  
Sorry, to be such harbinger of bad news! I felt you'd want to know.
 
Respectfully,

From: Alafair | Date: 2005-03-21 01:07:07 UTC
And then there is always the practice of civil disobedience with the
possibility of secreting those illusive WOM boxes with clues that
are hard to get one's hands on (read: not posted on line for NYS
Park officials, et al, to access) in various NYS parks a la the way
the game is played very successfully, I might add, on
f*e*d*e*r*a*l*l*y owned properties. With the large number of NYS
Parks and NYS being a comparatively nascent entry into North American
letterboxingdom, New York could be a hotbed of WOM
letterboxes,certainly an interesting regional twist to the game
compared to published box rich surrounding states like CT, MA, PA and
OH. But this is prolly just wishful thinking on my part.

In my experience, there is really no action to take when it appears
the sky is falling on others as in the geocachers. By being *pro-
active* and bringing up questions about how these regulations might
impact letterboxing draws unwarranted and unwanted attention to the
game by bureaucratic talking heads who perhaps previously didn't even
know it existed or at least never found the need to *regulate* it
while it was unobtrusively taking place under their noses.

If new or proposed NYS Park regulations clearly apply to geocaching
only, then the geocachers will have to deal with those
regulations...or not. If and when the regulations apply to
letterboxing specifically and then go on on to impose the same or
different regulations on letterboxing specifically, then letterboxers
can choose to comply..or not, or they can attempt to demonstrate to
disinterested bureaucrats whatever differences they can come up with
between letterboxing and geocaching and try to engage some change to
regulations vis a vis letterboxing or they can quietly engage in the
above-mentioned civil disobedience and keep their clues and boxes WOM
(and placed thoughtfully, of course) and off the radar screen.

Alafair

I hate to say this, but from their point of view, they may have
already 'regulated' us. Note the following paragraph.

"Multi-caches leave clues to find the next site, eventually
leading to a cache. Multi-caches will be limited to a total of five
(5) sites, but require only one permit. However, since the potential
use or impacts of each sire is the same, no more than five (5) sites
per cache owner will be permitted in one facility at one time. For
instance, if a multi-cache consists of 5 sites, the cache owner will
only be allowed one permit at a time."

Hiding plastic containers in the woods that require clues to find?
Can you say 'letterboxing'? The difference between geocaching and
letterboxing may be far too subtle a distinction for them to bother
with. If it's a plastic container in the woods, it's a geocache and
needs a permit.
