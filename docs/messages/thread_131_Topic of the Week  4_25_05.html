I used to use only Strathmore pads to the tune of $1.81 each.   ( I have a connection at ACMOORE and can get them for a discount).  Then one day about 3 weeks ago, I was minding my own business… driving home from some kid activity or another… certainly not thinking about logbooks, when I had a OMG! Smack the forehead DUH! moment…

 

So off I ran to WallyWorld and arrived back home an hour later with a 500 sheet pack of colored cardstock and a 500 sheet pack of white cardstock and about $7 poorer (poorer is that a word?) ANYWAYS I got out the paper cutter and started a-cutting.    Another hour later, I was off to Staples to harangue the poor copy center staff yet again with one of my hair brained ideas and test the comb binding and spiral binding machines.

 

The result was 23 logbooks in varying sizes from 2 inches by 2 inches for my microboxes and as large as 4 ½  inches by 5 1/2 inches just for the heck of it.

 

To sum it up, I used only about 40 sheets or so out of 1000 and spent… ARE YOU SITTING DOWN??? Well, of course you are, why would you read your e-mail standing up... silly me… J  I spent $4.88 to bind 23 logbooks! Some had as few as 15 pages, the thickest had 50 pages.  Comb binding is good for postal boxes as they are not as bulky as Spiral, which look a lot nicer.

 

Here is a link to a photo of a few of said logbooks… Now you know my secret! I’m never buying a logbook again!!!

 

www.alphabetbandits.org/logbooks.htm

What kind of logbooks do you use?  Do you make your own, if so what are they like?

I make ours and use this method for most of my "book" logbooks, a bit more personalized than the Strathnmore sketchpads we started with, and they can be made to size.

I love tinkering with images in PhotoShop and will use these for log book covers which I print on regular copy paper or cardstock.  For the back cover I have a pattern border leaving the center white for the stamp's image and box name, etc.   

It's all stapled together with a heavy duty Swingline stapler (Not Staples brand!!!) which handles thickness from 1/4" to 1".  I use a Logan matt cutter left over from my art school days to cut the pages for the book from copy paper,  it does the job in a jiffy.

After the book's stapled together, stamped etc, the front, back cover & spine are covered with clear contac paper for protection.

Here's what one looks like http://www.catbeadmischief.com/images/logbook.jpg

In the future there may be a fancier logbook put out with special wom boxes, but as a rule I try not to make them so precious they would be painful to lose.
