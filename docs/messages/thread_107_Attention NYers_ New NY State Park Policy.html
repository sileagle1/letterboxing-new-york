Wow, this is a lot to digest. I've only started looking through the
material, but it strikes me that there may be some good things that
come out of it, despite things like quarter-mile separation
requirements. Namely, if the NY state parks regulate letterboxing, at
the very least we know that what we're placing is "safe," so to speak.

While i realize many people are already responsible about getting
permission to place boxes, i have to wonder--as a result of things
like this, will LBNA and AtlasQuest and any other websites that
aggregate box placements now start providing links to state
regulations in their FAQs (if they're not there already)? Just
curious, and asking this with all possible protection for LB'ers in
mind.

Anyway, i definitely need to finish reading everything before i
comment any further and unintentionally insert my foot into my mouth
(if i haven't already!).
