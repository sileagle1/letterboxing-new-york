From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-09-05 04:15:25 UTC
I feel your pain. I have about 10 that are missing or destroyed ( out
of 81). I got smart and started saving my stamp drawings so I could
duplicate a stamp easily. But it's true that once the journal is
gone.....

My worst one was a little wooden suitcase that I had painted and
lined with maps and put travel stickers all over the outside. I had
hoisted it high into a tree and tied off the string around a nail
embeded in an old tree. Well I didn't choose the place wisely as it
turned out to have a LOT of kid traffic. They took the box but
luckily dumped the stamp and journal so I got those back. sigh

My box at Thornden (up near SU) is always going missing too. I will
probably have to retire that one as I am tired of replacing the
journal. I am getting better I think at placing boxes as it's mostly
my FIRST placed boxes that disappear. I avoid busy parks and stick to
ones with not as much foot traffic. I avoid playgrounds or ball
fields like the plague! And I try to place so that a spur trail will
not be formed and lead muggles RIGHT TO MY BOX. Camoflage tape has
helped too.

Don't give up as you are an EXCELLENT carver and I have really
enjoyed your stamps. Take a break and regroup and go over your hiding
places. I personally LOVED the last box in the Clifford and Lucy
series but I did wonder if it was going to be vandalized as it was
rather in the open. Ah well. A pity there are always going to be
jerks out there that will ruin anything pleasant.

So tell us..how is Lucy?
