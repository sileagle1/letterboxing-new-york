From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-01-15 21:07:47 UTC
>What do you tuck in your backpack?

Let's see,
In the personal stamp department:
All in one baggie:
-a tin holding our personal stamps,
-a handful of small ink pads in a variety of colors.
-possibly a little baggie with small pieces of card stock.
-both our logbooks.
-sometimes a 6x6" square of thick, dense
cardboard cut from the back of a pad of paper to
use as a firm base to stamp on.

In the box repair department:
a large baggie full of:
-bunches of baggies of different sizes (freezer,3x3", 4x4",3x5",4x6"),
-folded paper towels,
-one or two Rubbermaid containers,
-several centrifuge tubes with empty logbooks.

In the first aid department:
-Cutters bug spray, full enough for at least 2 -3
(or more) good spray coats (skeeters love me).
-band-aids, antibiotic goop, latex gloves,
Band-aid® Antiseptic wash with lidocaine,
Lanacane Anti-itch Creme (works wonders on
contact dermatitis), Ivy Block lotion (in poison
ivy season), Tecnu (also in poison ivy season),
Purell hand sanitizer (tiny little refillable
bottle), single packets of wet ones, Aleve.
-elastic bandage for that pesky ankle.

In the clue writing department:
-map to the park we're in.
-several sheets of folded paper.
-several mechanical pencils, ink pens don't work
very well with bug spray smeared on the paper.

In the camera department:
-a bottle of lens cleaning fluid sealed in a baggie.
-a pkg. of lens cleaning tissues staying clean in a baggie.
-a whole bunch of extra batteries because they
always give out at the worst time because I
forgot to recharge them.

In the everything else department:
-compass on a loooong string that's tied to my
backpack and tucked inside to keep it from
swinging all over the place.
-pedometer, on a leash.
-small packet of tissues.
-rolled up plastic grocery bag.
-small yellow bodied flashlight, yellow so I can
see it because by now as you might guess my pack
is pretty full.
-2 bottles of water.
-a hat or 2.
-clues.
-maps which might actually be where we're going....then again maybe not.
-light wt. jacket, if needed.
-neat sheet if it can be stuffed in.
-Chicago tool.


I'll be the one carrying the house on my back.
Cheers,
catbead

Re: Topic of the Week 1/17/05

From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-01-23 01:38:31 UTC
Ok, I'll bite for this one. I think we'll do as much boxing as the
snow will allow, that is, if it's not very deep. I can't imagine
finding much under a heavy layer of snow. I'm sure there are more
intrepid boxers, but I don't think I could deal with boxing in 2 -3
feet of snow.

We've hidden our tracks by doing a lot of extra walking around and
mussing up of the snow. Footsteps leading off here and there, looping
back, meandering about. Wiping the snow off the whole log, plus other
logs near it, etc. Scruffing the snow with a branch. Taking the dog
for a little "walk" off the path... You get the idea. Then there's
the old yellow food dye in a squirt bottle of water for a fake
nature's call. Wasn't it Snoopy who said don't eat yellow snow?
