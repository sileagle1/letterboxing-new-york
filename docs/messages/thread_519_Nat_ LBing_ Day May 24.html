Nat. LBing. Day May 24

From: pblber65 | Date: 2006-05-16 22:10:50 UTC
Got a person carving a stamp for me to put out on this day, maybe two
stamps. Went out today, scouted the area, and placed the neccesities
close at hand. This box(s) will be in Waterloo, NY in memory of
Memorial Day which started out there. (Can't believe no one beat me
to this one) Hoping others will be planting too. LW (YOU RULE)
prolly been planning this day for the last few weeks. I can't tell
any more WO counting but as of last week NY was only behind CT by
about 350 boxes. When are we gonna catch them. Gotta ask GT why he
changed that part of the site. It's still possible to get boxes for
the entire state but they are not numbered abymore.

Re: Nat. LBing. Day May 24

From: Cyndie | Date: 2006-05-16 22:59:41 UTC
Great job, PB! Yes, I have noticed that New York is quickly catching
up to CT. CT will probably always have the distinction of "Most
letterboxes per square mile" but I am so proud of the work of NY
boxers. Especially Scout in the southern tier and the Wizards of Oz i
n the north! Today I went to FINALLY place the Howland Island Box,
Tall Tails (awhile ago I had hiked in the 2 miles then realized I had
left the box in the car. Not wanting to walk a total of 8 miles for
that one plant alone, I decided to come back another day) and was
thinking about how a certain talented boxer told me she was thinking
of planting a series in the area. WHich is great...at 3,100
acres..there is plenty of room!

Then I went to spend some time FINDING and chose to do Craftymouse's
series of Accidental birds at Esker Brook. I have a box here too so I
could do a little maintenance as well. Well, what a fantastic series!
I don't know ANYONE who carves birds better than Craftymouse. The
detail is amazing. And her handmade logbooks make me green with envy.
If you haven't done this series yet, I highly suggest it. Between her
5 boxes and my one...you could get 6 boxes in a lovely 1.5 mile walk.
AnAnd there is always so much wildlife here. I have seen families of
rabbits and white tail deer...as well as all the lovely birds.

Nice job, Craftymouse!

LW
