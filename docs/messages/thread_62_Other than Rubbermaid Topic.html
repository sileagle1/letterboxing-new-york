Other than Rubbermaid Topic

From: paulonthepark | Date: 2004-12-26 18:17:39 UTC
I used a watertight plastic container from American Science Surplus
for my unique underwater Atlantis-themed box, which was in a
waterfall in Golden Gate Park (a temporary box - 2 months). I
planted it for our Golden Gate Gathering, and it was fun seeing the
faces on the people who found it as they returned to our picnic site.

Should you attempt an underwater letterbox, here are some tips:

I needed to use a heavy lead weight and monofilament line, because
being waterproof, the box floated like a bubble. The monofilament
was attached to the railing of a small bridge over the waterfall
using a clasp, and was barely visible and easily concealed. When
found, it could be pulled up out of the water, unclasped, and taken
to a nearby spot to stamp in. All of that worked fine.

What didn't work:

After staying perfectly dry for about a month, someone allowed some
sand or grit to get into the rubber seal, which allowed a small
amount of water to get inside. Even though the journal was in a
Ziploc bag, they didn't completely seal it, and it got damp and
spoiled. I added a note asking people to keep the seal clean in my
clues. I should have just put the stamp inside, with a laminated
note revealing a different, drier location for the journal in a
separate container nearby.

Look at all the ponds, lakes, streams, canals, and rivers that grace
NY - they're letterboxing opportunities if carefully thought out and
tested!
