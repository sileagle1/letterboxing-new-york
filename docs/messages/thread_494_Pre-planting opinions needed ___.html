From: songchick | Date: 2006-04-17 02:04:24 UTC
I'm really tempted to do a series that would be
located in various cemetaries here in the Binghamton
area. Nothing located around the graves or
masoleums, but a lot of our cemetaries have wooded
areas or fields on the grounds that would be good
places to hide boxes. Not to mention some of the
great stones that we have ... real works of art in
some cases.

Before I go ahead and carve (already have about 4
stamps designed) I was curious as to what you guys
think about the location. This is in no way meant to
disrespect the dearly departeed. I've just always
found cemetaries very neat, and great places to enjoy
a quiet walk and some meditation time.

Songchick

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-04-17 12:15:23 UTC
Songchick -
 
I  have 8 boxes in cemetaries ( for example, check my  Taphophile Series...a taphophile is someone who studies and enjoys cemetaries) and they have become some of my favorite boxes. They are usually quiet and private...perfect for boxing. Knowing you as I do, I trust you will be respectful and place boxes carefully without disturbing the actual grounds.
 
It's fun and interesting  to use gravestones as clues..and I have done this a lot in my cemetary boxes. I say, go for it!
 
Lock Wench

From: thespiritofmrpigeon | Date: 2006-04-17 12:44:37 UTC
I agree with LW. Cemeteries can be really great places to plant as
long as it's done respectfully, and not near any graves, etc. We were
looking for a spot to hide the Seneca Hill Ghost letterbox and the
only area we could find was in a very old, historic cemetery. The
stones and mausoleums in that place are amazing! I love seeing old,
weathered stones and thinking about the interesting people that are
buried there. What kind of lives did they lead? Plus it really
increased the creepiness factor on the Seneca Hill Ghost letterbox!

Squeak,

I usually consider carefully before I search boxes in cemetaries and go with my gut.  Sometimes i get creeped out or an odd feeling .  If that is the case I'm gone but otherwise I don't have a problem.  I've noticed in clues that are in cemetaries that that fact is noted up front.  I believe any box like that should be noted so that the individual can choose for themselves.

Paul, I have several cemetary boxes (check my Taphophiles, Unite!
series). Taphophiles are admirers of cemetaries, tombstones, and the
like. I have used that method (guidence by tombstone inscriptions,
shape, etc) and it has worked very well. My "As the Crow Flies" is
also in a cemetary. They are good places to hide boxes as no one
really bothers you and they make for nice peaceful walking. No wonder
people used to spend their weekends in cemetaries in the old days.
