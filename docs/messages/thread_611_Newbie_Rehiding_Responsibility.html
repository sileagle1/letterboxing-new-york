Newbie/Rehiding/Responsibility

From: thespiritofmrpigeon | Date: 2006-10-16 14:56:29 UTC
Oh boy. I see the big debate about newbies has appeared again. I'm not going to toss in
my 2 cents right now, but I thought it might be a good time to share what happened to
one of my series of boxes just this past week. Since we live in Oswego, we don't get to
check in on the Accidental Birdwatcher series in Montezuma very often. I got notification
on AQ that someone had found all but one of the boxes. I started to worry. When
Dandelion and I went down in the summer to swap out the full logbooks, we found 4 out
of 5 boxes exposed/moved/not-quite-right in some way, shape or form. Anyway, I was
worried about these boxes. Then this week I got a found notice via LBNA from a new
family. They had been walking on the trail at Montezuma and had found one of my boxes
COMPLETELY EXPOSED. They opened it up, read the information, and ---get this----
followed the directions!!!! They took the time to read all the info in the box, including the
notice begging the finder to please rehide well, and they did just that. They went online,
read about letterboxing, and a few days later, they returned with stamps to find the
complete series!

I immediately responded to their email, thanking them for taking care of my letterbox and
welcoming them to "the obsession".

Sigh. It was an email that made me feel really good. I'm so glad that I put all of the "what is
letterboxing" info in ALL of those boxes. I'm so glad that the family that found it was
caring and courteous. My faith has been restored for now.

Squeak,
Craftymouse

From: johnoballou | Date: 2006-10-16 19:56:21 UTC
Wow, guess there HAS been some messages on here in the last couple of days.

Here's my two cents.

If you find a dead body on the trail, how did the person die? What's your first gut instinct
in the cause of death? Someone killed them right? Dead bodies don't just show up,
people don't just die.

Well, now slow down and think of all the possibilities....
allergic to bee stings, tree hit them, fell out of an airplane, killed themselves, starved to
death, heart attack, choked on something, struck by lightning, poisonous snake bite...

...ok you get the point.

Now, you find a letterbox that has no natural camoflage (which is why I try my best to
paint/tape box anyway) why is it not rehid well? Well, yes, a NEWBIE could have done it,
but what are the other possibilities...

local kid found it and left it there, other muggles discovered it, squirrel or other rodent
disturbed it, heavy rain/wind uncovered, earthquake, tree fell on it (happened to my
sister), smelled good to a raccoon, dog off leash uncovered (one of my pet peeves)....

...ok you get the point.

So, Miss Moon, I'm glad you have an opinion, and I'm glad we have a forum for you to
share it on. Lockwench, I'm glad you have patience and a willingness to nurture
newcomers. There is no right answer to these questions, so we have to live with the
mysteries.

Why can't your roomate clean up after themselves?
Why can't a person not slam into your car with their shopping cart?
Why can't people stop completely at stop signs?

Because they are PEOPLE....we are imperfect.

To paraphrase..."We can recarve it, we have the PZcut!"

From: Bobby | Date: 2006-10-17 15:06:12 UTC
Well put. I've been reading these alot regarding newbies and rehiding
boxes and I've been wondering myself if it's really always a newbies
fault. I make it a point to re-hide boxes as well, or better, than I
found them, but if something happens after I leave that uncovers the
box, I'm not responsible. Except for the fact that my name is the
last one in the logbook, so the next person to locate the box is
automatically going to assume that I re-hid it poorly. Point is,
don't go off on someone until you know all the facts.

From: jiggs11onlb | Date: 2006-10-18 15:40:49 UTC
As a follow-on to Miss Moon's comments...

I recently checked one of our mystery boxes up here in ON. This box
was hidden at the base of a wooden piling blocking a dead end road,
in a small lock n lock. The box was at leat 8 inches beneath the
surface of the road, was completely covered in dirt, secured under a
flat rock that covered it and 2 larger pieces of broken pavement.

Imagine my surprise when I checked to find the box intact, with dirt
still encrusted, baggies and log intact, but the stamp GONE! The
finder had left a nice note in the log, stating he had "stumbled upon
the box accidently and left his email address and taken the stamp as
a "prize", even though DO NOT REMOVE CONTENTS was plainly displayed!!

Instead of packing up and pulling the box, I simply carved a new
image and carried on.

Yeah, people will find just about anything, no matter how well hidden
we think it is. All the more reason to ensure we rehide well, to at
least give the boxes a fighting chance.

Cheers, Jiggs

Ah heck, I don't even see poor rehiding as an exclusive newbie
problem (though I'm sure there are many newbies that don't hide
well). I could tell you about a very well known, to remain unnamed,
long time boxer who doesn't always hide properly and you'd probably
be surprised.

Found (finally) one box that this person had rehidden in a completely
new place! Now the clues said something about this box being hidden
under a certain particularily recognizable end of a log. This person
actually lifted the log to rehide it on the opposite end. Now, you
don't just forget when you found a box in a nice little cubby on one
end, but had to lift a large, long log to put it under the other end,
that this wasn't the hiding spot.

How did I know it was this particular person? They were the only
ones to find the box to that date. Sometimes the need to be the only
one who has found a particularly difficult box makes people do
strange things.
