Nice try, Lisa...but the birds will have to take care of themselves
for awhile! I am very busy carving stamps for our upcoming February
event...Desperately Seeking Sun. My son had surgery this week
(everything went fine) and I spent the time in the waiting room
carvingaway like crazy. I must have been nervous...because I have
carved 7 stamps in the last 48 hours! lol

Seriously, things are looking up. I will have the exact event venue
nailed down next week; after I check out 2 more places. I am trying to
get us as much "bang for the buck" as I can.....Great hiding places, a
great meeting space, easy directions, plenty of parking and extra fun
things to do. Stay tuned..

As far as my Report from the Road, it took me a few days to recover
from my last outing. LOL. I went to the Mohawk Valley to "capture" the
stamps of two of our most talented carvers; Lisascenic and Paul in SF.
I had a wonderful time getting more of Paul's bird series...especially
since I am from that area. I knew exactly how to get to each of the
box
locations! My last of Paul's boxes was one at the Parkway in Utica and
was hidden near that imposing bronze eagle that overlooks it. I
discovered the park road was chained at that hour but as I was a local
I knew I could still reach the park area by going through the cemetary
next door and through a pedestrian gate on the top of the hill. So up
I drove...only to discover the pedestrian gate was locked/chained as
well! *sheesh* Too close to my prize, I decided to climb the fence. It
is a 6' heavy duty cast iron fence with 6" sharp spikes at the
top..but as I was wearing my brand new skateboarding sneaks
(excellent tread) I
decided to go for it. No problem on the way over...found the box and
stamped in..then headed back to the fence to get back to my car. I
madeit up the fence...and over..then as I went to jump down, my new
sneak
caught between two upright bars. I fell, but never touched the
ground..and my poor right ankle took my entire weight. There I
was..suspended from this fence. My left foot couldn't quite touch the
ground..only inches away. My right leg was bend behind and above
me..my poor ankle shrieking with pain now. I finally arched my back
as much as I could and grabbed two of the spikes behind and over my
head..and hung there..taking a bit of the weight off of the ankle
while I wriggled my foot..trying to free it from the shoe. With tears
streaming down my
face, I finally got out, pulled my sneak from the fence, and collapsed
with relief onto the front seat of my car. A couple of walkers looked
over curiously but moved on. I am sure they were wondering what the
heck I was doing on that fence!

The ankle was much relieved now as well, and I decided to call it a
day. I drove to Ilion, picked up my dad, and we continued to Little
Falls where we attended the Annual Dinner for the Mohawk Valley
Alpiners hiking group. After a few cocktails, I couldn't feel my ankle
at all! I really enjoyed the speaker..a fellow named Bill Ingersoll
who is taking over the writing for Barbara McMartin, the godmother of
the Adirondacks who just died from breast cancer this summer.

Of course, by the time I got home my darn ankle was the size of a
grapefruit so no more letterboxing for me! Lisascenic's boxes will
have
to wait for another trip. *sigh*

In the meantime, I am very gratified at seeing how busy the I Love NY
Hitchhiker Hostel has been. Be careful to mix your tracks if you visit
it this week...we've had a bit of snow!

Happy Thanksgiving, everyone -

Lock Wench
