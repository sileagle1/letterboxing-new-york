From: AC | Date: 2004-10-25 21:03:03 UTC
Hi Cindy,
 
Welcome to boxing!
 
The Alphabet Bandits have 3 series in the works... two partially planted and one in the carving stage.  Each one has a theme:
 
The Hudson Valley Alphabet has 26 stamps... each in a different type face and each representing a cool place to visit in the Hudson Valley... I - Indian Brook, P - Poet's Walk, etc.
 
In the I Love to Read Series each stamp is the main character from children's books... Junie B. Jones, The Littles, Cat in the Hat etc...
 
The other is Legends of the Silver Screen and each stamp is a caricature of a film star... Lana Turner... Jimmy Stewart... John Wayne etc.
 
None of our series have images that are directly related to the name of the place in which they are planted... and many of the ones we've gotten weren't either. 
 
How about a Veteran's series... maybe you could do a image to represent each of the major conflicts in which our soldiers have represented us... the US flag with 13 stars...the Pearl Harbor Memorial... should be relatively easy to carve.... a WWI helmet...  ... the images don't have to be similar, and the only one they need ot make sense to is you!  I think that would actully be a very nice series and would prompt people to visit all the parks.
 
I learned from our alphabet series, that I'd spent YEARS! driving past the most amazing places on my way to the most boring things duties.
Anyways I'd love to hear how you decided where to place boxes, what to use for a stamp, and how you come about the names. Thanks in advance, Cindi

From: Scott and Kathy Trefz | Date: 2004-10-25 23:09:41 UTC
Cindi:

Well, having a father who is a WW2 vet and having served in the famous 101st
Airborne Divison, it would be very easy for our family to do a series for
vets - I would have my husband carve a screaming eagle for one and then for
the others I would do like another Lber suggested and have stamps that
represent other branches of the service or a stamp to represent each time we
were at war starting with the Revolution and including the Civil War to
current. We just finished a series of 4 Lb's on historical little out of
the way spots here in Madison County, NY and each of the stamps represents
what happened in that spot (well, except for one). We have a lot of rich
Native American history in this county.

But I want to be a witness to what AC said in his post. We "built" a
vacation AROUND letterboxing in the states of VT, Mass, and NH and we saw
some of the GREATEST little out of the way spots that would NEVER show up on
a tour map or travel brochure but thanks to Lbers in the area, we saw and
learned some fantastic things!!!! The best vacation we have had in a long
time!

Go for it!!!

Kathy
Ma of the Pookah Clan
