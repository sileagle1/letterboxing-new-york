Team Dakota got us all excited about these containers called Lock &
Lock. They pop up on sale at places like Wegmans and Quality Markets,
and they come in reall cool sizes for LBs. They have a little flap
that comes down and locks into place on all four sides and they're
very sturdy, as far as we can tell. The best part is you can get them
on sale for like 2 for $3 bucks, usually.

I'd post a link, but I'm not about to get into the whole "commercial

From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-01-02 02:05:05 UTC
Thanks Paul! :-)

My all time favorite container is the centrifuge tube. They're small
(about the size of 2 film cannisters) yet hold a stamp of 1" x 1 3/4"
and a logbook 2 3/4" wide, cheap (2/29¢), survive temp. extremes of
-70ºF to +212ºF and are waterproof as long as the caps are screwed on,
extra easy to hide (therefore lowering the risk of accidental
discovery), small enough to tuck into a pocket when planting on the
trail, and best of all...they don't weigh down the backpack. Can you
tell I'm in love?

Happy 2005!

catbead, on a mission to cover NY with centrifuge tubes.
