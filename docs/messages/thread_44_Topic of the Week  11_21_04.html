From: catbead1 | Date: 2004-11-26 04:48:02 UTC
> How did you first learn about letterboxing?

About 2 years ago our paper had a 2 page spread on letterboxing
complete with color photo's of local boxers with their logbooks and I
think a letterbox. The article was sympathetic to the needs of
letterboxing, one look and I was hooked. Unfortunatley it came out in
winter and we had to wait a few months for everything to thaw, so
every so often I'd pull the article out reread it and say to Fly Doc
"we really have to do this" and he'd nod in agreement. Finally, the
weather was nice enough and we were off. Luck was with us and our
first find was in one of the most beautiful locations I've had the
pleasure to visit. Our second try was a box that had been MIA for
some time but was still listed on LbNA, in spite of that the day was a
lot of fun and I came away with some great photo's (one is on my
Puzzles page).

Cheers,
catbead

The Highlander and I seem to be in some disagreement about this. I
keep thinking I saw someone's geocaching website that mentioned it,

crummy day the day before and was working into another one when I
suggested we go see if this letterboxing thing was for real. We
finally went searching on August 29, 2004 and found our first 3
boxes, all Elba Onions' plants and 2 of them first finders. We didn't
even have our STAMP yet, but that was enough to get us going. I
planted our first box the next day and my second a day later. Haven't
looked back since!
