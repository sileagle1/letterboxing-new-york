The potholes in the rock outcrop in West Canada Creek, three miles northwest of Nobleboro are
examples of the grinding power of volumes of water swirling small rocks, gravel and sand. In this
case it was --- and still is --- Adirondack rock grinding Adirondack rock that created these
unusually large granite-gneiss potholes. http://www.paulkeeslerbooks.com/Chap3CreatedMV.html

I summered in the town of Ohio as a child so this got my curiousity going. We used to fish in an
area northish of Hinckley Lake from hugh cliffs on either side of the West Canada - route 365. I
was ten so don't remember if there were any potholes but in doing some research found the above
information. Nobleboro is farther north - take 365 to route 8 and turn left. From the information
I found there are probably more of these potholes along the canada but probably not accessable.
HTH - mama b




I tried both of my long distance friends (NYC & Key West) to see if
they remember how to get to the Potholes on West Canada Creek. We're
all getting up there in years (over 50) and memory is foggy, even
though we fondly remember going there to swim in warm pothole water on
occasion.

MY SUGGESTION: get a USGS Geologic survey map of the area, and look
for a dam, a power plant, and plunging ravine topography. The maps
will include roads -- even farm roads. There's also a bridge nearby.

I wonder if the new online satellite photo/maps would work.

We want to go to Moss Island when I visit last week of this month.
Would someone please post directions?
