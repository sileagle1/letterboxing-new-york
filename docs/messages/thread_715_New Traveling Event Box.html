From: AC | Date: 2008-01-29 19:20:16 UTC

Hi All,

 

I’ve attended many gathering over the years and have always been amazed at the varied and wonderful dishes boxers bring to the pot luck table.  This weekend the star of the Winter Games: Box O’lympics was Firestarter’s hot Mexican layered did, which turned out to the main course for many of us.

 
The Amazing Butternut Soup I had at Fright Night II and the dip (both of which I cornered the maker for recipe) gave me an idea for a traveling event box.  Do you have a favorite item that you like to make and bring to gathers for the pot luck table?  If so e-mail me off list.  All you need to do is send me the recipe with a little history as why you bring it or why it represents where you live and carve a stamp to go along with the recipe.  The recipes will be compiled into a booklet which will travel with the stamps and be available to anyone who wants one.  I’d like to have recipes from all over the country!  What do y’all bring to gathers down it Texas?  How bouchas up in Minnesota EH?  Dude what’s cooking is Callie?

 

Although I didn’t make it for the Winter Games, I’m going to put in my recipe for Egg Cream, a real New York treat! And probably my Maple Espresso Cake.  What are you going to include?

 

Talking Turtle

Of the Alphabet Bandits

Of the Hudson Valley

Negative space was: New Traveling Event Box

From: AC | Date: 2008-02-02 12:41:03 UTC

When you are learning to carve, there will be a LOT of experimentation.  I’ve been boxing and carving since March of 2001, and I still have to experiment.  In fact I’m doing  positive/negative trial carve right now. 

The key to a good carve is a good original… unless a drawing is line art (strictly black and white, no grey) I edit it in a graphics program.  I use Adobe Photoshop CS2 (only because it’s the program I happen to have).  You often have to play with an image to get something that is crisp and has enough contrast and therefore is “carveable”.  The hour it takes sometimes to manipulate an image to get a good original is well worth the effort.

Now, when you say carve negative, are  you looking for an image where the white creates the image or the black?  I’ve created a folder (talking turtle) in the pictures folder that has examples of each.  The Sin City image is one I carved for Fright Night3.  Sin City is a graphic novel in the Film Noir style, which lends itself nicely to carving.  In both of these, the “white” was carved away.  In the Sin City image, although the white is carved away, it’s actually the white (or negative space) that creates the image.  Unlike the Disney image, in which the white is also carved away, the black creates the image as in a traditional stamp.

Using negative space to create a positive impression creates a very dramatic stamp.  The stamps tend to be very simple images, that have a lot of impact.  Orange Marilyn was the first stamp I ever saw that used this technique. Although it creates a great image, too much of it can be a bad thing.  These images benefit from an ultra crisp original and a steady hand.  Otherwise the resulting image will look blurry.

I will sometimes, invert an image to create an original like the one used for Sin City, but not every one works well.  I have my fair share of those as well.

I hope this at least in part answered your question, maybe you can attend a carving demo in these parts (the Hudson Valley) one day!
