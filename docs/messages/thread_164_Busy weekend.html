Busy weekend

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-05-31 00:27:07 UTC
Whew..that was a busy weekend of letterboxing! I planted 8 more,
including one in Herkimer County, worked some on two more series for
the Syracuse area, plotted one for the Adirondacks this weekend, and
searched for 3 boxes (only one of which I found, but found out later
the others had come up missing for other people as well.

Posted my boxes on Atlasquest as well...ordered some carving medium
online, and searched for info on potential hiding places.

Wow, this hobby makes me forget all about drinking! (just kidding)

Re: [LbCNY] Busy weekend

From: Jen Z | Date: 2005-05-31 16:18:21 UTC
My letterboxing weekend started off a bit disappointing when I went to Commercial Art Supply on Saturday to pick up some carving material... and it was closed! Ack! Off to a BBQ in Liverpool, then I swung up to Michael's in Mattydale.  They didn't have any carving stuff either! Oh I was very disappointed! :o(  However, I did manage to do 3 of Catbead's letterboxes at Highland Forest Sunday. I was so excited that I overshot most of the clues but had a wonderful time! Now I just can't wait to get to Syracuse again this weekend... hopefully Commercial will be open so I can get some of my stamps finished and planted!

Commercial Art Supply is Soooo Expensive! We have another Michaels a
mile or two down the road on Erie Blvd. just east of Thompson Rd.

Mall). Go in at the light where you see "Toys R Us". Off hand I
don't remember if A.C.Moore has carving material, but I did buy
Speedball cutters there sometime in the past year.

From: Cyndie Long | Date: 2005-05-31 18:57:16 UTC
Jen - I have never seen carving material at Commercial Art. Have you bought it there before? I go to Commercial Art for the little black books that you might have seen in some of my boxes. I also buy some small linen covered spiral sketchbooks there as well. I passed on the info to the manager there and they promised to start stocking more of the books. I guess I should have requested carving material as well. The Michael's in Mattydale doesn't carry carving stuff but the one on Erie Blvd carries Speedy Cut (which I bought before I knew better) and the pink stuff.. Better call before you go, however...as they never seem to carry much of either. I buy my pigment type ink pads there as well. I just recently ordered some of the Master Carve from Mister Art online. I got too frustrated looking for the stuff locally. Let me know how you make out.
