CNY sitrep

From: dagonell2001 | Date: 2005-08-20 22:28:03 UTC
Just got back from vacation across NYS. Went to visit MIL in Syracuse
on 8/12. Found "@ Cat" (First finder!), The "Wonders" of Onondaga
Park (And I nearly busted a gut laughing when I found the first box.
I won't give away the joke, but it's worth the hunt just for the
joke!) [all 3 found and final puzzle solved], made an attempt at "Your
Ad Here" but the building closed before I found it. Made an attempt
at "Trio of Visually Impaired Rodents" but discovered the entire area
being bulldozed and re-landscaped and gave them up as a loss without
even trying to find them. Went after "I Never Strike the Same Place
Twice" and found only the first two. The first one was a challenge
because there were fishermen all over the spot. I took out my stamp
book, sat down and pretended to be sketching. I slithered along on my
butt, until I had 'paced' off the last measurement. I stamped in,
right under everyone's noses and nobody realized I wasn't really
sketching! Number three in the series, eluded me. A certain tree is
now a stump and I'm afraid the box might have been picked up with the
branches and limbs. And someday I want someone to dissect a litterbug
to find what makes them do it. It's an utterly gorgeous park, but the
amount of litter made me want to make someone walk the plank! 8/13
went back to "Your Ad Here" I was perusing the shelves when a voice
behind me said "Now there's a suspicious looking pirate!"
Craftymouse, Dandelion and Tarheel were after the same box. They gave
me clues to a few more Syracuse boxes, but I only had time to nail
"Cairn" before leaving the city. Drove to Coxsackie (just South of
Albany) to meet with old friends and crash at their place. I printed
out all the letterboxes in Greene county where they live, but didn't
bother to work them out on the map. I made two mistakes. 1. They
live at the northern edge of Greene county, all the letterboxes were
down in the Catskills, another days travel. 2. The weekend I pick to
be there was a Noah's Ark, you gather the animals, I'll build the boat
rainstorm all weekend. We stayed in and schmoozed with our hosts all
weekend. 8/17 we stopped again in Syracuse on our way home. I got
"All Natural" and "Pirate Gear Required" I was slightly miffed that I
was second finder on Pirate Gear by one day! What would have been
more appropriate than a pirate being first finder on a pirate box? :D
I spent the rest of the day in Clark Park hunting down "Cave
Paintings", both groups! Five miles of hiking over rugged terrain! A
certain letterboxer went after the series four days prior to me, and
if we meet at an event, I'm going to dress them down. The Caves
series were very poorly re-hidden, two were out in plain sight and one
wasn't even recapped! It hadn't rained, so there was no damage, but I
made sure that all 12 were re-hidden well. I remembered the locations
for "It's Good for the Dog, Walk" and "Happy", and checked on those as
well. Crafty mouse and Dandelion told me that a box had been added to
"That Darn Cat" series, so I went through it again. I was surprized
to find that the boxes had been moved, so this was a little harder
than I anticipated. Particularly, since I had already put in five
miles on Cave Paintings. Couldn't find #4 in the series, however I
didn't have my compass and had to 'guestimate' the degree reading, so
it may be fine. Found the other 5, plus the new box. And how was
your week?
-- Dagonell

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-08-21 03:39:59 UTC
Whew! I am exhausted just READING your schedule! I found "@the Cat"
right after you and I kept hoping you had been first finder
on "Pirate Gear Required!" What would have been more appropriate?? I
STILL haven't found that one..even after catbead corrected my
bearings. I think the biggest problem was I went back at dusk and
realized I didn't have my flashlight with me. I did see 3 deer at the
crossroads just ahead of the box area and spent way too much time
watching them. By the time they moved on it had suddenly gotten very
dark. Oh well..just another reason to visit!

Glad you liked the Wonders of Onondaga park. That was my first ever
series. Can't you tell by the LINED notebooks I used??!? Sheesh. The
Trio has been retired and a new box planted with new clues under
Furnace Brook Trail. The Corcoran students worked very hard on this
new nature trail and have made incredible progress. I still have the
Trio letterboxes so maybe they will get replanted somewhere else. I
will check on the third box of "I Never Strike Twice" just to make
sure it hasn't been trashed. Thanks for letting me know.

I had a good weekend scouting spots. Also planted two new boxes...but
not too far away.These gas prices are really putting a damper on
letterboxing. grrrr...

Lock Wench
