After printing up hundreds of clues and usually returning with 10% or
less actually FOUND, I'm thinking the same as the Onion folk are. Box
series' are the kind of thing you THINK your body can handle, but
reality sets in and you have trouble finding a box or two, so in
essence, you get about 3, maybe 4 good hours in, but no more than
that in a day. Think about the 19 boxes at the Ravine...those are
fairly close together and that STILL takes a good 2 hours. Imagine
them spaced FURTHER apart and that'll be when you start finding our
bodies on the trail, one hand stretched toward
that...elusive...second...box...(gasps the final gasp and is still).

10 miles of walking alone will make this into a few trips for sure,
expecially if your clues get trickier as you go. It's a neat idea,
but definitely not a daytrip! :-)
