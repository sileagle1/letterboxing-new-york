topic of the week

From: mickeybarber | Date: 2004-11-23 01:09:46 UTC
In the Times Union Sunday paper there was a full page article on
letterboxing and we knew it was right up our alley. We like to do
moderate hikes and walks with our minpin and I love puzzles. The
article was in the paper the last weekend in October and we got our
stuff together and began. We've only found two boxes, just very busy
with life. Now that hunting season has begun and they've already shot
a school and two other hunters, we probably won't be going into the
woods. Time to figure out where to plant and make stamps. Now that I
know more(a little) the article actually gave away one location which
is a no-no, but I'm glad we saw it, and began this hobby. Wayne, Lynn
and Mickey in upstate NY

We bought some inexpensive blaze orange fleece snap-on vests at Dick's
that work well for us. Hunting is permitted in lots of the state parks
so we're avoiding them completely right now, but we wear the vests
even if we're on the Finger Lakes Trail or in parks where hunting is
prohibited. Not every hunter is a conscientious or law-abiding hunter,
plus there's no way for us to know if the trail we're on happens to
border private hunting property (and there's plenty of that around
here).

In addition to wearing the blaze orange, we also avoid
wearing/carrying earth tone colors, especially browns, blacks and
white, when we're out hiking at this time of year. I usually run
screaming from garishly colored wardrobe choices, but come fall and
hunting season, the brighter and less natural-colored the better!
