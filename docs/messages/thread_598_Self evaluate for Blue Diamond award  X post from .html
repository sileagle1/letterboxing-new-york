Blue Diamond excellence award or popularity contest?
Just spent a rainy Sunday afternoon looking at the most prolific
planters in the Hall of Fame P100 club. Some of these folks have no
diamonds. They may have chosen not to participate, or their boxes have
few visitors or maybe they are just average or below average box
creators, no way of telling. Some of the P100's have lots of diamonds
esp if they have a travelling series, each box in the series gets a
diamond. The folks with the most diamonds not a part of a big series
are Green Tortuga, LightninBug, True Indigo, and Amanda in Seattle. I
know it is not supposed to be a competition but if one offers "Awards
of Excellence" to a limited number of people it IS a competition. Or
is the intent to really help boxers locate outstanding boxes? If so, I
have a suggestion that is a kinder way of identifying really good boxes.
I can understand that some boxers may appreciate the blue diamonds if
they are on a limited time budget and value the perceptions of other
boxers. I like many other folks have chosen not to be evaluated in
this way. Some of my boxes are really good and I have received lots of
positive feedback on them. Other boxes I have created are OK. Soon I
hope to find some of LockWench's excellent boxes, none of which have
blue diamonds as she too has chosen to opt out of this feature. As my
time will be limited I will ask her which ones she recommends. Why not
allow folks to self evaluate? I would be much more likely to trust a
planter's opinion of their best boxes than someone who may or may not
share my criteria for excellence. Each boxer who wishes to do so could
identify their 3, 4 or 5 personal best boxes with a blue diamond. Or
maybe a percentage of their plants if that's not too complicated. I'm
not asking for another feature! Just tweak the existing one to return
AQ to the kinder friendlier site that it once was. Share the diamonds,
there should be lots to go around. Nor

Re: [LbNY] Re: Self evaluate for Blue Diamond award X post from AQ

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-10-01 22:33:22 UTC
First of all, if no one wants to read my objections YET AGAIN about the BD controversy, please skip this post. 

LB-

I have never even found any of your boxes except for a particularly fetching cootie and a great HH but I would venture a guess that you are being lauded for your clue writing skills. I have gazed with wonder at THOSE.

Not all great boxes have great stamps or great locations. It's one of the many reasons I don't like all this. First and foremost, I think rating is the beginning of a horrible trend in modern letterboxing. I now can empathize with the British as they watched those horrible Yankees post clues online! 

Secondly, rating boxes is too subjective. . And it especially burns my butt that more "experienced" boxers opinions  ("experienced" in this case only means they have logged their considerable finds on AQ) are measured against people who don't have a lot of finds or don't log them on AQ, and their votes are given more "weight." To me, that smacks of elitism.  I was MOST incensed that this feature was originally slapped on all our boxes without our prior input, without our permission, and with no recourse. It was only after several of us complained about having our boxes "hijacked" for this little algorithim experiment that Ryan capitulated and let us opt out.  Norasta, you might have missed that one!!!

For those reasons, I have opted out. But I also thought a lot about potentially driving too much traffic to certain sensitive bd boxes, potentially increased maintenance of bd boxes, and potentially putting them at risk for someone wanting to "collect" bd stamps.

I obviously have had more than my time at the mike to voice these personal opinions on AQ..and I have agreed to sit back and let the system work. I suspect that Ryan just had a lot of time and inclination  to develop this system and see if it could fly. I do admire his willingness to challenge himself, and he has obviously designed a complicated system that he is justifiably proud of. I just wish he would  play with his own toys and those of his close friends, and not MY boxes. To his credit, he has agreed to let people opt out of this experiment.  People have a perfect  right to decide if they want to be a guinea pig or test case and to grade the boxes of neighbors, friends and children. I say, no thanks.

LW

Re, your Leprechaun....it sounds cool! Several of us have Blue Diamond themed stamps on deck. Can't wait to see them all!

LW
