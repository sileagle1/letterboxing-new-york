Well, I took a young'un out for her first boxing experience and
thought I'd share!

My partner at school was planning to box with me last week, but had
to work her other job last minute. Her daughter was bummed and I get
along pretty well with kids, so I carved up a stamp and offered to
take her.

I'm tellin' ya right now, a kid on a boxing run is an absolute MUST,
espeically if you're digging around for Celtic Quinn and the Cosby
Kids' (including Moonlightgamer) boxes! LOL High schoolers and
college kids seem to believe there's absolutely nothing wrong with
sneaking around school grounds and in bushes behind people's houses
and such, but as a fully grown woman, I am not ABOUT to be arrested
for letterboxing! So, young Princess C, on her first boxing venture,
did quite a bit of box shagging while I sat in the car and waited to
drive off and leave her if the cops came (now, don't get mad...I'dve
told her mom where to post bail!).

Anyway, we first hit my Lock City box, only to find out that it had
been walled up behind about a foot of spray on concrete (anyone truly
determined to find this one should bring a jackhammer!). We also
checked on "If Only For A Moment" and this one is officially gone.
Not to be deterred in showing my young charge a good time, I took her
to Williamsville to hit PomPom and Celtic Quinn's Boxes by the Glen.
Pom Pom was easy, but the other two had so many folks out enjoying
the day that we had to abandon the search.

From there, we went to the most distant and interesting place I could
think of to take a kid from Lockport...the Theater District. We did
pick up Knitica's new box and then, on a whim, dashed over on our way
to Kenmore to get my Weebls Footy Badger for Princess C. Finally, we
cruised back through Kenmore to get Jaguar Pride and one of The
Plumber's Tools series, all of which were still around and quite
safe. Celtic Quinn's Jaguar Pride seems to have been found by a
teacher at the middle school, though...they stamped in with a
generic "Excellent" teacher stamp and signed in saying "KMS STaff has
found you!" (oooo, somebody's BUS-TED!)

All in all, a great day! We found two hitchhikers to boot, so
Princess C's new total is about 7 boxes, I believe. She was
thoroughly delighted (and I have to say, even I felt like I had found
a great boxing pal next time the Highlander is busy), so if you see
her update on a box of yours, be sure to send this eager 9 year old a
thank you...it'll really make her day!

The (lazy, but still boxing) Sprite & the (had to work that day)
Highlander
