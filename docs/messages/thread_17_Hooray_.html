From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2004-09-30 20:09:53 UTC
You're most welcome! I'm very glad to see Central NY come so alive with
letterboxing! I do have heartfelt connections having grown up in Utica, and
going to college in Brockport before moving to Boston. It seems funny to be
moderating and promoting this group from almost 3,000 miles away, though.
But I have my own somewhat selfish agenda, and here it is:

1. I have niece and great-nephews that I'm glad to see break away from TV,
and get outdoors to do something fun and creative. I got them started, and
they're having fun. Having a bigger letterboxing community in the area,
makes it more fun and exciting, not just for them, but for the whole area.
Let's face it: in much of NY, there aren't enough recreational activities
for young people, and they end up "hanging out", bored, either in their
neighborhoods, or at the mall. Letterboxing is a great alternative.

2. Every year, I visit my family, and I want to have some fun in Central NY
myself!

However, if anyone local wants to take over as moderator of this group, I'm
sure that the Northern California Letterboxers Group (LbNCA) would cheer, as
I'm running behind in some promised collaborative work, such as boxes
Vertigo #2 and #3.
