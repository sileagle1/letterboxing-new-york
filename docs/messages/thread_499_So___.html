Well, there was no slapping involved, but I was spotted by LOSTGIRL
fairly early on. I guess I confused some by wearing my hair up. But
Jackbear certainly was in heaven. Attendence at this convention was
about 200 to 1, in terms of females versus males. The event stamp
commemorated this....with an image of 5 women in bikinis surrounding
a male and... Well, I think I'll make this one a personal traveler
or something....too cute to leave in a drawer. When I arrived just
before the door opened, there were about 200 people in line already.
While chatting with many in line, I learned that the majority were
scrapbookers, with rubber stamp card makers making up the rest. I met
no other letterboxers, but many were intrigued by the idea. I gave
out the website addresses to maybe 3 or 4 people.

It was a really nice group though..about 8 of us. We commandered a
large circular table and passed stamps around. I got a chance to give
Craftymouse the Desperately Seeking Suds stamp that she missed on the
night of Knitica's car crash. I cheated and piggybacked on Jackbear's
responses to get Craftymouse's personal travelers. Awesome stamps and
presentation. My Ex-in-Box was let out for a little air too. Wyvern
carved a cool new signature stamp for LOSTGIRL...be sure to do an
exchange with her. So clever.... We had a newbie in our midst and I
am hoping that this smaller and friendly group tricked, I
mean "convinced" her that we're all relatively sane. *grin* We also
had a rather striking young couple walk up to us and ask us if we
were letterboxers. We happily replied yes and they said they were
letterboxers in the Ithaca area. They gave us their names, and when
we asked what their LB names were, they said they had stamps but no
names yet. Hm... I wish I had remembered to invite them to the Ithaca
gathering!

So anyway, we bought cool inkpads and lots of neat paper and other
stuff. I passed on the comb binding logbook making machine but was
informed by Maiden1974 that there are similar, much cheaper machines
on ebay for about $25. I looked for a commericial stamp maker but no
such luck.

We had plenty of time to talk and make eye contact, etc...which made
for a very pleasant time. I kept thinking of my friends up in Mass,
meeting for the next day's Ice Cream Social with over 125 people
signed up. I dunno, but I am thinking we had the much better time....

Missing you, Lisa......much love to you and Robb.

Lock Wench
