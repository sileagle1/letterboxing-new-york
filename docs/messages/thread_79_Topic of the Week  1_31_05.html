From: dagonell2001 | Date: 2005-02-01 23:52:43 UTC
New Year's Resolution: When Grad School starts up again, get organized.

Grad School has started again, and I'm organized.

TOPIC OF THE WEEK(S):
> How did you first learn about letterboxing? 11/21/04

On Livejournal (www.livejournal.com). A friend of mine posted in her
'blog about this cool hobby she had found but hadn't yet tried out. I
followed the link back to the LbNA website and tried it. When I went
out to visit her several months later, I got her hooked on it. :D

> So far what is the most unique letterbox you have found? 11/28/04

The Kimball Library box. Most of the others have been straightforward
directions. The Kimball box has been the most intense puzzle box that
I've solved to date. I vow to do my own puzzle box sometime.

> This was recently on the newboxers list and generated a lot of
> discussion. When do you think a boxer moves from 'newbie' to a more
> seasoned 'oldie'? 12/05/04

When this first came out, I joking referenced that the cut-off date
was 18 months, making me the only local 'oldie' at the WNY gathering.
:D Seriously, I think it's the first time you think, "What the?..
Okay, it's the same as ..." the moment when you draw on a previous
letterboxing experience to walk through a new puzzle.

> How did you come up with your trail name and stamp? 12/12/04

Dagonell the Pirate is my SCA persona. The SCA (www.sca.org) is a
hobby organization for people interested in the re-enacting history.
I've read pretty much every book in Buffalo on pirates. :D My stamp
is pirate-themed.

> How do you come up with the names for your boxes? 12/19/04

They're themed on either the stamp I've carved or the place I've
hidden them.

> Other than Rubbermaid, what other types of containers are used for
> hiding letterboxes? 12/26/04

I have found film canisters but haven't used them yet. The new
Taster's Choice coffee jars are perfect, but I don't like their
coffee, so I'll have to continue pulling them out of the recycle bins.

> Camouflaged boxes. Have you found any? Do you create them, and how
> do you do it? 01/02/05

Found any, no. Created any, not yet. One I'm simply going to paint
jet black so it blends in. Another is ... well, let's just say I'm
inspired by Poe's "Purloined Letter" or the movie "Hide in Plain
Sight". :D

> What do you tuck in your backpack? 01/09/05

Letterbox clues, ink pad, personal stamp, colored pencils, magic
markers, compass, map, hitchhikers, spare baggies, water bottle.
Stuff like driving directions, first-aid kit, and fire extinguisher
get left in the car when I park.

> Now that winter's here do you plan to do much boxing? Have any tips
> to share for disguising your tracks leading over to the box?
> 01/17/05

I tried in mid-January. The sun sets too early to make full days of
it. I used yellow water in a squirt bottle to disguise my purpose. :D
Going the day before a large snowfall helps too.

I'm spending my time, designing my stamps and clues. I'm planning a
series along the Chautauqua Rails to Trails property, 30 miles of
trail to hide multiple boxes in, and I have permission from the trail
manager to 'GO FOR IT!'

> What's your favorite boxing experience? 01/24/05

The Eternal Flame. Earth, air, fire, and water all in one isolated
spot. Later this year, when it's warmer, I'm going back just to
meditate in that spot. :)

> Do you tie history and letterboxing together? How do you do it?
> 01/31/05

As often as possible. Usually my clues contain the history of the
location, or the history of the topic of the stamp.
