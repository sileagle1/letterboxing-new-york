Planting boxes question

From: ducacj | Date: 2005-04-06 13:24:50 UTC
I'm trying to plant 4 boxes we have made. I have someone in the
area who will be checking on them for me and then probably removing
them?? for the winter for me if we don't make it back.

I have the stamps carved, the history research done and written,
and even have an idea of how I want the clues to go.

HOW do I go about actually planting the box? Should I just go pick a
place to start the walk and then write clues as I search for a place
to put it? Should I find the place to put it first and then work
backwards?

I'm planning on asking permission I forsee no problems with that.

Someone posted on taking boxes with them to plant in another
location. How do you know what is a good place to put the box?

All those kinds of things. Any help would be greatly appreciated. I
have to say I love the stamp carving and am really hoping in the
next year or two to plant alot of boxes there and in my own area.

Cindi

From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-04-06 20:32:40 UTC
Hi Cindi,

First find your spot, a good spot & try to imagine if it's a good
hidey-hole even when all the leaves are gone. Then, after the box is
planted write the clues working backwards, double checking (even
triple checking) your right & left's (I can't tell you how many times
I've written down left when it's been right!), # of steps, name of
path, etc. Take lots of clue reference pictures so you have
something to look at when you're rewriting the clues. For example:
if a tree growing in the middle of the trail is an indicator that 5
feet beyond a small path is leading off to the left into a little
opening in the woods, and the box is hidden at the base of a "U"
shaped tree somewhere inside. Digital images are a big help, almost
like being there.....and you can be as straight forward or ambiguous
as you want when writing up the clues.

After the clues are written, if at all possible, go back and follow
the clues word by word to get the kinks out and see if there are any
mistakes or they need improving before publishing. If you can't do
it then quite often someone will be happy to test them for you.

Cheers,
catbead

Re: [LbCNY] Planting boxes question

From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-04-07 00:18:18 UTC
I have a non-letterboxing friend that I have try out my clues. I go along
with him, and just follow and watch, taking notes and saying nothing (unless
my clues are too vague -- which I want to know about & fix, anyway).
Usually, some confusion or false lead is good fun, as long as it's able to
be straightened out without major frustration.

I think it's better to have someone else test your clues, simply because
they're in your wording and writing style, so you might understand them, but
others might not. Making sure your clues make sense by testing them will
make people appreciate your boxes more.

Paul in SF
