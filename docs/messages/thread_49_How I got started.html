How I got started

From: songchick | Date: 2004-11-25 15:21:14 UTC
I got into letterboxing the roundabout way.

I take my daughter to the Saturday morning kid's craft class at our
local Michaels on a weekly basis. I got to be good friends with the
instructor, and while waiting for my daughter to finish up one
morning, I was looking around the room at the displays for upcoming
classes. I noticed a display on a stamp-carving class coming up.
The instructor (Carol) saw my interest and was talking to me about
stamp-carving. She mentioned that she uses her stamps that she
carves for letterboxing. So of course being nosy, I asked her what
letterboxing was. She filled me in on all the details.

When we got home, I looked up the website she wrote down for me.
Once I started reading, I was intrigued. I signed up for the class,
and found my first letterbox on the way home with my brand new
stamp, logbook, and inkpad in hand.

This was two years ago. We've been boxing ever since. And it's
been a life saver at times. I was diagnosed with lupus in February,
and have had to give up a lot of my activities due to the fatigue
and pain that comes and goes with the disease. But the one thing
that has stayed the same with no change is my letterboxing. In fact
my doctor encourages me to continue as it's good for me to get out
in the fresh air and hike. So letterboxing is not only a hobby, but
therapy as well!!!

Didn't mean for this to turn into a testimonial ... I'll get down
off my soapbox now. :D

Wishing everyone a happy, healthy, and festive Turkey Day.
Hopefully this weather will break so we can all get out and do some
boxing!!! But then again, this IS Upstate NY <snicker>
