From: ducacj | Date: 2005-02-17 19:53:04 UTC
We use an artists sketchbook too. I usually make 2 copies of the
stamp one to put in the logbook and one to put on a scrapbook page.

Our logbook is actually a scrapbooking album with page protectors.
It lets me add in the name, date, location and any other information
about the letterbox. I then do a 12 x 12 scrapbook page with the
second stamp and add in the clues, the pictures, and our thoughts
and comments about the box.

From: catbead1 | Date: 2005-02-19 02:07:01 UTC
We use spiral bound books found in the scrapbooking section of
Michaels, 6"H x 8"W and bound on the 6" side, 35 pages. The cover
folds all the way around to the back, making a firm stamping surface.
The paper is nice and thick so no ink bleed thru happens....acid free
too. I keep a seperate book of the same size for all the stamps I've
carved.
