Inspired by Lock Wench

From: tellicogap | Date: 2005-07-12 02:35:23 UTC
Okay ... if Lock Wench can do it, I can try! I did my first real
carving of 6 new letterboxes that I left on the New River Trail in SW
Virginia. They're not too bad, but the real reward was my pride in
actually getting over the fear of turning out a recognizable image.
So ... I have brought that new confidence home and have carved a bunch
of new stamps. I hid two of the Carousel series today and will work on
getting more out over the next week or two. So ... you can add
Binghamton to your destination list. I've decided to have a sort
of "tithing" philosophy ... hiding at least a tenth as many as I find.
I have a bit to go! Scout and the Miller family of Endwell, NY

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2005-07-12 03:00:17 UTC
WOO HOO! (Doing an awkward looking victory dance). I am so pleased you gave it a try! A few of my stamps are pretty bad, and a couple of logbooks even worse..but no one really seems to mind. I guess it really IS all about the location and the hunt! And I am happy to know that I can spend some more time in  the Binghamton area as well. I was so bummed when I ran out of time and had to blow throught there a couple of weeks ago. And I am sure the folks in  the SW Virginia area will be pleased with your additions as well. Nice job!
Lock Wench
