want to plant my first boxes-- please advise!

From: strawberryshortkook | Date: 2007-08-04 20:20:19 UTC
Hi everyone

I'm a docent (volunteer) at the Utica Zoo, and there aren't any letter
boxes planted within the zoo property. However, I think it would be a
great place to plant some boxes! However, there is a fee to get into
the zoo, and I'm wondering if it's still OK to plant boxes somewhere
if you have to pay to get in. What's the proper etiquette? I know
there are boxes planted at state parks, and most of the time, you have
to pay to go there, so paying to get into the zoo, when you can make a
day of it AND letter box, doesn't seem too outrageous to me.

Any suggestions would be appreciate! And yes, I am planning on
talking to the zoo director and any/all zoo keepers that would be
responsible for maintaining the areas in which the boxes would be planted.

Mary

From: Paul | Date: 2007-08-04 23:35:52 UTC
You could plant it just outside the zoo's property. That way, you
leave the choice open. should someone want to see the animals or not.
If you really want to put it inside the zoo, then clearly state that
there's a fee involved, and you might want to include the zoo's hours
so that you don't frustrate others.

P.S. I used to help out at the zoo in the early 70s.

Paul in SF

From: Walter W. Quinn III | Date: 2007-08-05 01:43:52 UTC
If you list your box on Atlas Quest, there's even an icon that you can select that tells people that it's a 'Fee Area'.
I'd list it in the clues as well, but it's certainly not against the rules to plant within an area that you have to pay a fee to enter.
People will just want to know that they'll need money ahead of time.

From: songchick | Date: 2007-08-05 15:21:37 UTC
Most definately plant in the zoo!!! I think there are
other zoo boxes around NY (I know of at least one in
Ross Park Zoo in Binghamton, but it's not in the zoo
proper itself). If you log your letterbox on
Atlasquest, there's an icon you can include that shows
it's hidden in a fee area. And if you put it in your
clues, then people can decide for themselves if they
want to pay to get in to get it or not. Personally, I
have no problems paying an entrance fee to something
if I can get a letterbox. It would make a great
family trip

Good luck!

From: Christine Benson | Date: 2007-08-06 02:21:08 UTC
Several years ago our family visited Longwood Gardens in PA. In the
main conservatory they had a "letterbox" hunt (un be knownst to them
though). I think that with the permission of the zoo staff it would
be a great asset. It could be used as an educational opportunity and
it would get people to the zoo ( $$ admission - good for the zoo). I
know there are some people who will do anything to get a find.
At the conservatory (it was aimed at children) you could pick up a
flyer that talked about six different plants. You needed to walk
around this HUGE greenhouse looking for the correct plant and then
when you found it stamp in. It would be nice if the seeker had to
learn something about the animals at the zoo in order to find the
stamp. While finding boxes is fun, I love the puzzle aspect of some of
the clues.
Hope this helps and hope to see some boxes at the zoo soon.
Mama B of the gypsy travelers.
