There are varying ways I hide my boxes.  Most of the time it’s rocks and leaves… I’ve recently begun experimenting with camouflage of various sorts.  Paints, burlap.. etc.  Krylon sells a paint called Fusion that sticks to plastic really well.  I’ve started with the paints because a couple of times I’ve walked up on one of my boxes that wasn’t hidden quite as well as it should and to me (maybe because I was knew where to look) stood out like a beacon of blue plastic sticking up out of a pile.

 

It also depends on where the box is located.  A couple of times we’ve had to “import” rocks because there just wasn’t enough “stuff” laying round to cover it.  I prefer though, a nice pile of rocks with a few twigs and leaf debris.

 

I just recently hid a box from myself… took me almost 40 minutes to find it… 15 to remember where I’d planted it and the rest trying to find it’s hiding place.  Drew a total blank… and here I was walking around in circles more of less talking to myself…thank goodness it was a seldom used trail.

I have been buying cheap silk leaves, mostly ivy-looking-ish, and hot gluing them to the lid and sides of the box. This helps a lot in most instances in giving me hiding options. I figure that as they age and discolor, they'll still work as camouflage. One youngster, upon finding my 'Star-Crossed' box with his mother, remarked, "Wow, Mom! Look -- he used salad"!
 
You can make faux rock surfaces using Granitex, or Premo or Sculpey polymer clay, and embedding dirt and small pebbles into the clay. The surfaces have to be baked separately and then hot or super-glued on to the letterbox to avoid melting the plastic box during baking. The legendary letterboxer, Sir Balthazar, gave me one of his faux hollow rocks that he makes using some industrial materials and are time-consuming to make. I think Granitex polymer clay works as well, if not better, than what he used (his broke on me). He hadn't thought of using polymer clay. You could make 1 or 2 good hollow rock molds to create any number of 'faux rock boxes' from, and keep them on hand. Sir Balthazar has a faux rock box that's been in plain view for years and hasn't been discovered by the public. If you want more info on making molds, let me know & I'll refer you to some good polymer clay technique books.
 
I like to hot glue enough wire onto the box to allow it to be attached to things more spontaneously on the spot. It works well for stabilizing and increases your hiding options greatly. You can then attach the box by wiring it onto the back of signs, branches of trees, etc. Use a wire that won't rust - or is plastic coated.
 
One last really unique and totally fantastic idea that I found on a box in Long Beach, CA, but never tried. This box's hiding spot was a piece of black PVC drainage pipe that had some sort of lidded cover that fit perfectly over it. It stood upwards, coming out of the soil, and placed under some landscaping foliage near the side of a building, it really looked like something functional for drainage or plumbing. No one would question it at all. Very easy to access and replace -- and it scores a full 5 star rating for clever disguise!
 
Paul in SF
