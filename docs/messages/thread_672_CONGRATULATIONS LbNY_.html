CONGRATULATIONS LbNY!

From: Paul | Date: 2007-07-23 11:14:40 UTC
Congratulations LbNY on out Third Anniversary!

When I started the group in July 2004, we were a tiny group of only
10 members, and since then, we've grown to a whopping 130 members!
This group is now almost as big as LbNCA, the group I belong to in
Northern California, which has been in existence for a much longer
time.

I now see boxes the have been planted in many new areas that didn't
have a single one in 2004 or 2005. And many of you participate in
gatherings in the state, which I hear are successful and a lot of fun.

Since I only visit once or twice a year, I don't get to do as much
letterboxing in NY as I'd like. But I do manage to find some boxes in
central NY where my family lives. It makes me feel good to know that
starting this group was a great idea, since it's brought so many
people together for a wonderfully fun activity that beats sitting in
front of the TV all the time!

High Five to everyone in LbNY!

When visiting and leaving your heart in San Francisco, be sure to
hunt for a couple of my boxes, too!

Paul in SF
LbNY Founder

I am having fun letterboxing, but unfortunately, I don't have enough
time to get around to as many boxes as I'd like. We've been focusing
on the Oneida, NY area, which has so many boxes I've nearly been
tripping over them every time I take a STEP!

To LbNY members: Just a friendly reminder to consider a couple of
things when you're planting:

1. Be mindful of placing boxes in poison ivy area. I'm lucky -- I'm
immune to it, but a couple of boxes were smack in the middle of the
itchy stuff, so my family couldn't go to the boxes and had to send me
in.

2. Boxes planted on steep slopes without anything strong keeping them
from sliding downhill will most likely get washed away in heavy
rains. I noticed some boxes that were in danger of that, or were now
missing because of that.

Since you put so much work in on planting a box, don't place in
vulnerable spots or places where folks won't go.

** I'd like to tip my hat to Party Pig and friends for their numerous

for numbers of boxes, but it looks like others have been inspired by

Sorry I have to leave so soon, I could keep myself busy planting &

In my home territory of San Francisco, I'm ready to plant a set

gem - Highly Recommended" icons on AtlasQuest. If you visit, look for
them, as well as my other favorites: "Star-Crossed" and "Dias de los
Muertos".
