Thanks for asking about raffle donations, Chris. A wish list of raffle
donations would look like this:

1) Sheets of PZ Cut or Speedy Stamp (the pink stuff)
2) Lock n Lock boxes or similar containers
3) Tiny letterboxes
4) Rolls of Camo Duct Tape, heavy duty freezer ziplock bags...pint size.
5) Small logbooks
6) Maps (check with AAA, you are allowed so many free maps a year)
7) An inexpensive compass
8) Carving tools/blades such as #1
9) Gasoline Gift cards
10)Colorwheel ink pads or markers specifically for stamping like MarvyB
11) Randy Hall's book on Letterboxing


Basically, everything a letterboxer might want or need....or could give
to encourage another person to become a boxer! Donations can be as big
or as little as you wish. Some non-boxing "kid" items are always
appreciated too. I have donated "wacky" items before just to break up
the monotony. But bottom line...ANYTHING you want to donate would be
greatly appreciated. Chocolate, a lawn ornament, a cd...ANYTHING AT ALL
will be used and appreciated.

Lock Wench

They are veterinarian hypodermic needles sizes
#16 or #18 , much too big to be used on humans,
that I got from our Vet's for I think 25¢ each.
The sharp edge is used as if it were a tiny
X-acto blade, or use the point to make
holes...but don't use it like a gouge because it
won't work. :-) Scoutdogs discovered this
little tool a few years ago, and if you've ever
seen her stamps you'll know she's a master
carver, they are fantastic. A picture of a
needle is at the bottom of this page following
her carving tips:
http://www.scoutdogsboxes.com/shopping_page.html

Re: [LbNY] Re: Raffle Items

From: Paul Gonyea | Date: 2005-12-10 08:49:48 UTC
Try using your standard tools differently, too -- quick shallow flicks of
the narrow line carving tool can give you textured shading. That works for
'pitting' the surface. (Used that technique for the gauze-lace effect of the
hat in my Day of the Dead stamp, Lock Wench)

I imagine that's what the needles accomplish for MtNestRobin. You could also
try using skewers, or heavy gauge wire to make scratches and hatchmarks.
Also try GENTLY squeezing your narrow v-shaped tip CAREFULLY with pliers.
You can get a slightly finer line. (Too much pressure with the pliers, and
you'll snap the tip).

Paul in SF
