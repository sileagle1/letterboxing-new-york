Royalton Ravine -- sitrep

From: dagonell2001 | Date: 2005-01-15 15:48:08 UTC
After being housebound with three days of rain, I was getting cabin
fever. On a beautifully clear, but cold Friday, I bundled up, ran
several errands and drove out to Royalton Ravine to finally tackle the
"Thankful" series that had been planted for the 1st WNY Gathering. I
had been too busy socializing at the gathering to actually letterbox.

Unfortunately, after all the errand-running, it was already 3PM by the
time I arrived at the park. I found the first 10 in the series,
realized that that beautiful orange-red sky to the west was actually a
sunset and tromped back to my car by 5:15. It was dark by 5:30.

The first 10 boxes are all alive and well. No first aid needed. A
few folk from the gathering had come back a few days after the
gathering to stamp in, so apparently I wasn't the only one too busy
socializing to letterbox. If you think it was muddy and slippery in
November, you should have been there after three days of rain in
January. I had on three layers of clothing and I was still chilled.
Interestingly enough, we've apparently gotten a boy scout troop
interested in letterboxing. They were through just before Christmas.
They didn't have a stamp so they used the stamp from each box to
stamp in and signed their troop number below it. Someone must have
been carving as they walked, because later boxes in the series have a
crude stamp that the earlier boxes didn't have and they were dated the
same day.

10 down, 9 to go. A good day of letterboxing, not a great day, but a
good day.
-- Dagonell the Pirate

sure if we'd be able to leave early enough to have a few hours of

to show that standing around the woods stamping in makes us think

we nabbed. I harbored hopes of doing all the Ravine and the other
