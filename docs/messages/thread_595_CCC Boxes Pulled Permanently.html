CCC Boxes Pulled Permanently

From: Jen Z | Date: 2006-09-27 12:55:01 UTC
Hi Everyone,

Last night the pups and I went over to the CCC Nature Trail in Auburn to check on my "Lucy and Clifford's Wildlife Adventure" series that I have hidden there. I meant to just check on it, but I found 4 of the 9 boxes either badly re-hidden or not rehidden at all... just left out in plain sight or wedged into an area and not covered. One was just dropped behind a boulder, instead of rehidden in the nook underneath the boulder and then covered with brush. Grrr. Because this is a high-volume trail, I decided to permanently pull this series. I'm sorry for the lack of notice. I meant to pull them in November if they were left out like my other series in Auburn, but when I saw how lackadaisical the hiding was yesterday, I decided not to wait.

I did not have a chance to change my website to reflect their retirement, so I wanted to let the list know.

FYI, I took a picture of one of the boxes which had been re-"hidden" by being wedged into the lowest branch of a tree... in full view! Before it had been in a hand-sewn camoflauge bag, up in the well-firred evergreen boughs. I posted it in my blog:

http://trailhoundz.blogspot.com/

I'm a bit peeved, to say the least. How HARD is it to re-hide a box, as good as or better than you found it?!?! *sigh* :^{

Again, sorry for the lack of notice. Thank you to all the boxers who found and enjoyed (and rehid properly!) this series previously... I had a great time carving each image and really enjoyed everyone's signature stamps and comments in the logbooks. I know Auburn is quite a hike from Syracuse and from surrounding areas, so the fact that many of you took time to drive out and look for them is very special to me. Thank you! :o)

~Trailhoundz (Jen)

From: Tracy Baxter Haynes | Date: 2006-09-27 15:29:57 UTC
I think Jen is being very diplomatic here.  The truth is these boxes were found by some new boxers just yesterday morning and she came along and found them exposed that evening!  It wasn't as though these boxes sat out for months without being found so that time and weather possibly had an opportunity to expose them.  These boxes were exposed strictly because someone didn't care enough to take the time and effort to rehide them properly.
 
This was a great series and now it is being pulled and retired.  That is a shame.  These boxes were planted on a very highly traveled trail but they were hid properly.  Budgie and I found this series at the beginning of the summer and during the summer, they were found numerous times.  None went missing.  Now, because of someone's lack of care, they are pulled. 
 
If you have found this series, her Outwit, Outlast, Outplay or Bark at the Park boxes, you are familiar with how she made camouflage bags for the boxes.  How the heck does a camouflage bag for a box go missing?  You know that you found the box tied in a tree in a bag.  And then you just stick it back on a branch?  Walk home with the bag?  She also told me that me that one of the stamps was missing from a box.  Hey, I know stuff happens, you can get distracted when you are stamping in and trying to be stealthy (that last 'stealthy' part can be read as sarcastic if you are so inclined  :-)) but how do you walk off with a stamp???  And if you do, what?  You just keep it, add it to your own collection?  C'mon people!  Either make a return trip to replace it or at the very least notify the planter and offer to send it back to them. 
 
What should she do?  Notify the last finders and tell them?  Educate them about proper rehiding?  The problem with that is it is reactive instead of proactive.  She is lucky that she came along when she did, because on that trail, those boxes, hidden the way they were, would have been gone in a day or two.  I am all for educating new boxers, I just don't see why our boxes have to be sacrificed to do so.  One thing I have learned in the past year is that no one thinks they rehide poorly.  I even know of one boxer who when notified that the boxes they found last were left exposed claimed they were 'sabotaged'.  Right.  Someone came along, found the boxes you supposedly rehid so wonderfully and just exposed them, taking nothing, doing nothing with them.  Just found them somehow, even though you did such an awesome job hiding them, and left them in the open. 
   
Sorry if I sound a little harsh but I find this extremely disappointing.  This was a nice series, in a nice little location, with nice stamps.  And now it has been pulled.  Trailhoundz has done a great job in making Auburn a worthy destination for a letterboxing daytrip and I hate to see this happen to her boxes. 
 
~artichoke (a little grumpy this morning and feeling fed up and very offended on behalf of my friend!)

This is what I wrote to the last finders. I hope it sounds okay... I don't want to make them feel embarrassed or anything but hopefully give a little educational nudge about checking your hiding. :o(

>>Dear --,

Thanks so much for taking the time to come out and look for my boxes. I'm glad to hear you enjoyed them.

I really hesitate to even mention this, but I actually visited the Nature Trail boxes for a check up yesterday evening and probably only missed you by a couple of hours. As I went to check the letterboxes, I noticed that 4 of the 9 had not been rehidden very well. Two of the 4 were fully exposed with no cover over them. I just thought I would send a friendly reminder to rehide letterboxes thoroughly after finding them, as good as, or better, than you found them. :o) Sometimes it's hard to remember because you get so excited to go to the next box, but an exposed letterbox risks being found by non-boxers & stolen, or becoming litter. A lot of times, if I find a box exposed or half-exposed, I'll search around for more sticks, rocks, debris, etc. to put on top, adding to what was originally there. Afterwards I check from all sides to make sure it's invisable and blending totally in to the environment. Stealth and secrecy... all part of the game! :o)

I noticed you are new to AQ... if you have any questions about getting around the boards, or about letterboxing in general, let me know and I'd be glad to help. I've been boxing for about 2 years now and enjoy it immensely. It's a great way to get outside, hike with the pups, and enjoy the great outdoors.

Have a great weekend!
Best Wishes,

or half-exposed, I'll search around for more sticks, rocks, debris,
etc. to put on top, adding to what was originally there. Afterwards I
check from all sides to make sure it's invisable and blending totally
in to the environment. Stealth and secrecy... all part of the game! :o)
