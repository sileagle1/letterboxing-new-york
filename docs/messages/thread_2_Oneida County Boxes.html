Trust me, planting boxes is fun, and really not all that hard. Pester the
manager at Michael's to carry the Speedball stamp carving supplies (they
used to, but stopped). I'll try to find & post the links of a couple of
places where you can order supplies. Instructions on carving are on
www.letterboxing.org, and it's kind of addictive, you get better with each
stamp you do.

I planted a box today that is my best stamp to date - it's of Shakespeare's
Juliet. It's about 4 1/4" square, split down the middle, and hinged with
duct tape, so it folds. I made a Popsicle stick brace which slides in to two
loops, so it holds it open while you stamp. This was an experiment that
worked, and allows me to use larger stamps and still keep my boxes small
enough to hide.

I'm jealous! I love Raquette Lake...

Rumor has it that there will soon be more Oneida County boxes (and maybe
Herkimer County boxes). Congratulations on completing the full set to date!
