I Am Disappointed in Atlasquest/Blue Diamond

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-09-24 12:09:30 UTC
Although I am usually a big supporter of Atlasquest because of it's
user friendly interface and incredibly responsive webmaster, Ryan
Carpenter (the Green Tortuga), today I was crushed to see a certain
new feature on the site.

First of all, I LOVE the new search features he has implemented and
the new limited time only icon! Now I can finally mark the couple of
boxes I have that are out for the holiday season only.

But I am appalled by the new "coveted" Blue Diamond feature. For those
of you who don't know what I am talking about, you can check AQ Post
#38869, and my own response #38874. I fear this feature will do more
to destroy this game than the hot potato called Publicity ever will. I
welcome all your comments on this board....and would love someone to
give me a fresh perspective. Keep it clean, but I really want to hear
how you all feel about this new system. Would you use it when you are
deciding which boxes to find? Would you use personal recommendations
instead? A combination of both? How would you feel if your box earned
a "coveted" Blue Diamond? How would you feel if it didn't? Inquiring
minds want to know....

LW

Re: [LbNY] I Am Disappointed in Atlasquest/Blue Diamond

From: songchick | Date: 2006-09-24 16:21:49 UTC
Here's my $.02

I DON'T like it. I carve and plant purely for my own
enjoyment, and to give others who share this great
hobby more boxes to go out and hunt for.

It's not so much earning the "coveted Blue Diamond"
that I have a problem with. I'm just plain
uncomfortable with the thought of other boxers going
online and rating my boxes. If someone doesn't like
it, if the carving is not the greatest (and believe
me, some of my earlier ones aren't!!!), I'm worried
that giving a box low ratings will keep other boxers
away. I liked the old system of asking on the local
boards for recommendations ... you get to meet new
boxers this way - at least I have. And a box that one
or two people may not have liked for WHATEVER reason
may appeal to others. With a low rating, they may not
be tempted to give that box a chance ...

I can understand the reasoning behind implementing it,
I just don't like it.

OK, I'm off my soapbox. Let the boulders fly

From: _the_eidolon_ | Date: 2006-09-24 19:04:31 UTC
"And a box that one or two people may not have liked for WHATEVER
reason may appeal to others. With a low rating, they may not be
tempted to give that box a chance ..."

--I think this mindset is what is confusing people. It is not a
RATING system in that way, it is more of way for finders to say "this
is the best of the best". There is no such thing as a 'low rating'
with it. If your box is rated 9.5 or 1.5, you or anyone else will
never know. The only thing you will ever see is a blue diamond if the
behind the scenes calculations rate it high enough. All it will tell
you is that people who have found this specific box say it is worth
making a point to go look if you have to limit your searching.

Re: [LbNY] Re: I Am Disappointed in Atlasquest/Blue Diamond

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-09-24 19:23:42 UTC
But it IS a rating system. People can put in a 1 or a 5 or a 10...even just depending on the mood they were in that day! I have some loyal friends that would put in 10's whether or not my boxes deserved it or not just because they want to thank me for introducing/mentoring/sharing letterboxing activiites. I have others that I am sure would give me all 1's because they think I am a crazy letterboxing freak! lol   My point being; without parameters or measurements, it's USELESS. This does not mean the I feel the system should be given criteria or parameters or measurements because the sample criteria cited can only be subjective at best. If we could rank size of the stamp or box, THAT would be precise.  Not a popularity contest.

I got into this hobby because I am a local history nut and an urban and wildlife explorer since my childhood.   I know a lot of places that are off the beaten track and some that are hidden in plain site. I enjoy sharing them with people. In fact, my favorite comment to receive  is..."WoW! I've driven by that a million times and never knew it was there!" I also found I like to carve. I like to carve WAYYYY too much and so had a real backlog of boxes to plant for awhile. I try to limit my carving now to gifts for specific people and/or events.

But the truth is, I spent my OWN money and my OWN considerable time researching, carving, making, camoflaging, planting and posting a great deal of letterboxes, the majority which are open and available to all comers.  Now my boxes are being marked by a system that I did not ask for...nor was my opinion sought out, and I am given no recourse to opt out of the system.

I don't like it. It's enough to drive me back into geocaching. :)

LW

From: _the_eidolon_ | Date: 2006-09-24 19:58:49 UTC
But how does this change how, why and where you plant? If I do a
search for boxes in Syracuse the extra icon just means I might want
to make it a point to look for that specific box. That does not
change the worthiness of the others. It is not like the 1 ratings
given by your notsomuch friends causes a lump of coal icon to appear
beside your boxes. All it means is enough people felt that certain
box was more special than most, for whatever reason. Yes, the ratings
of those who blindly give 1's across the board can skew the ratings
just as much as those who give 10's across the board. People should
not give their 'friends' high scores just because they like them,
respect them, want to thank them. That is the only "issue" I see with
the concept; people have to learn to give an honest opinion, which a
lot cannot unfortunately.

I guess I just see this entire concept as more useful for non locals
than locals since locals are likely to search for them all eventually
anyway.

Re: [LbNY] Re: I Am Disappointed in Atlasquest/Blue Diamond

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2006-09-24 20:28:37 UTC
I don't know if you noticed  my "practical" points on my AQ post, but that gives me pause as well. If one of my boxes WAS deemed to be a popular box, I am not sure I would like having to run to it constantly to replace the logbook or recarve the stamp if it became worn. I already work very hard to maintain what I have.

People have planted as many boxes as they did based on the system already in place. If this new system remains and someone found themselves with several blue diamonds (and some boxes I have found around here definately deserve a heads up), they will have to rethink having so many "available to the public" boxes around as they may not be willing or able to keep up with all the added maintenance that an announced  "popular" box would require.

And of course it would not be fair if some of my friends voted for my boxes because they like me any more than it would not be fair that some didn't because they DIDN'T like me. I meant I FEAR such a thing would happen. And this is entirely possible with a "behind closed door" system. . But if we kept things as they are ( without the rating system)...that point would be moot.

For recommendations of local boxes, non-locals can always post on the AQ state boards ( so easy!), and/or the big board..if you don't mind a little scorch mark on your hem. :) And you'll know exactly WHY a box has earned a big thumb's up from some of us.
