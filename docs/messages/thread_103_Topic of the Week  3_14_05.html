From: AC | Date: 2005-03-15 16:51:44 UTC

Were I independently wealthy, I’d use Staedtler’s MasterCarve exclusively.  It’s almost as soft as “buttah”, maintains fine line and detail beautifully and is thick enough that it needs no mounting.  On the other hand, it’s EXPENSIVE!  And for the number of stamps I carve, I don’t want to have to make a choice between carving medium and groceries.

 

My second favorite medium is PZKut, which is the only medium I use.  Like MasterCarve, it holds detail and fine lines extremely well.  It’s not quite as soft as MC but is easy to carve.  It’s a little tougher on the on the blades than MC, but for the price it’s work the extra blades or sharpening every now and again.  As for price… well, there’s no comparison.  I use Grade “B” and have NEVER had the ”slight imperfections”  that are supposed to exist effect a piece I was working on.  I can easily get 8 – 10 stamps out of a sheet or more depending on size.  It takes transfers very well, although it will melt if you are too heavy handed with the iron.  And what other company can you buy from on Monday and have your product 2 days later without paying an arm and a kidney.

 

Since I’m in it for the carving as much as the boxing and adventures, I appreciate the fact that I can indulge myself and not break the bank.  Thanks PZKUT Family!
