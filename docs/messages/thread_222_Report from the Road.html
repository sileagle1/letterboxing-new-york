Made the trek down to see my dad recently and planted some new Oneida
Country letterboxes: Oneida Community Mansion House (National Historic

be "maintenance week" and I have already retired some boxes
permanently.

Now just waiting for cooler weather to plant my Adirondack boxes!

Report from the Road

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-09-23 22:28:42 UTC
I had a very productive letterboxing day this week. I decided to make
it a "Lisascenic Day" and capture some of her stamp images now that
the majority of the tourists are gone. Most of her boxes are hidden
in my old childhood stomping grounds so I knew I wouldn't get lost!
First I took the Thruway to Canajoharie and went for her Canajoharie
box near the Academy. I didn't find it but only because the sidewalk
mentioned in the clue had been recently replaced. I checked every
nook and cranny but no box. Did anyone else find it recently?

Next I headed to the "potholes"...a swimming area we went to many
times as kids. I still got excited when I saw the traffic light that
is in the middle of the intersection. As a kid, that meant we were
really close to our destination! I found the Green Barn box with no
problem, then hopped in my car and headed south to Sharon Springs,
saving the other Canajoharie boxes in Wintergreen Park for another
day. After all, this trip was for Lisa's boxes!

Got into Sharon Springs and immediately went for the Healing Waters
box but there were still a ton of tourists milling about! I
remembered back to my first trip as a child and my fascination with
all the Hasidic Jews I saw here. The boys especially with their long
curls on either side of their faces..hats tight on their heads. I
decided to save this box for another time and went to the Block
Printing Nirvana box instead. What a fabulous stamp! I was so happy
to get this one and headed down Route 20 to get the Wild Strawberries
and Our Friends the Amish stamps. I was FF on the Strawberries...and
the location is one of the hidden treasures of NYS. My parents had
brought me here as a child and I know I made my mom nervous as I
scrambled all over the cliffs.

After the Amish stamp I headed down to Cooperstown and got the Opera
Series (I used to visit the Goodyear mansion next door as I went to
high school with one of their relatives) and then got the Secret of
Mirror Bay by Lisa and the Dream Team series by the girlguides.
Frank's box in the Dream Team series was obviously missing and I
didn't have time to grab the Brewery stamp...but I will be back! I
had a lovely drive back up Route 28...(repaved since my last time
here) and went down memory lane as I traveled down Vickerman Hill
into Mohawk. I grew up in the town next door and it was always
thrilling to hear about runaway trucks that were saved from disaster
because of the runaway stop ramp that had been built. Prior to that,
out parents used to regal us with stories about trucks spilling their
cargos of potatoes and oranges..and once a runaway truck filled with
TNT ran straight throught the town of Mohawk! whew!

Thanks, Lisa...for a great day. I thank the girlguides as well. Even
better...I had dinner at the Glen Loch and finally picked up the last
That Darn Cat stamp! woo hoo!!

Report from the Road

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-10-25 05:46:07 UTC
It's been especially challenging to box this week....as I haven't
mastered stamping in while trying to paddle a canoe. LOL This rain
keeps on coming but I give thanks that I don't live on the Gulf Coast.

Even with the rain..it's a good time to do maintenance. So off I went
to check on some boxes. I was thrilled to find that 2 boxes that had
been reported as missing are actually safe and sound. woo hoo!

I also moved my Furnace Brook Trail box after finding it right out in
the open twice. It is in a better hiding spot now. Be careful if you
do this trail though, as there has been quite a lot of flooding in
the area. You can easily double back on the trail rather than doing
the whole loop, however. I decided to "tough" it out..what the
heck..I am an experienced backpacker and all.... Bad idea. I got to
the stream and the temporary bridge was long gone. In it's place,
someone had thoughtfully places haybales, stumps, and logs. Each
separately..so you could choose your poison.

The haybales looked the safest to me so I started across.
Yipes..waterlogged haybales! My feet were now soaked as I was wearing
running shoes. I lept back to shore and eyed the stumps. They were
uneven and quite tall but I decided to try it. Eek! Even worse! They
sank and twisted on their mud foundations and I seriously saw my life
flashing before my eyes...so back to shore I went. The logs laid
crosswise certainly looked dry on top. And they were nice and even
and low to the ground..what the heck. I'll bite. Two steps on and I
realized my error. Have you ever seen those log rolling contests?
Well, imagine that with a middle aged woman on top. yow zah! I barely
made it across to the other side! I kept looking for the Candid
Camera guy to jump out of the bushes..I bet someone could make
serious money with that novelty footage. Of course, you would have to
edit out all the things I uttered while attempting to cross the
stream...

I'll be heading out to explore 3 Falls Woods ( in Manlius) a little
more before I plant there. If anyone has been there before and can
tell me how to get to the limekilns..would you please contact me
offlist at: lockwench@... Thanks -

So what have YOU all been doing during this rain?

Report from the Road

From: Cyndie | Date: 2005-11-04 15:28:58 UTC
OK..the I Love NY hitchhiker Hostel is up and running with 5 new
hitchhikers. It is an adapted from a design by Alafair...who planted
the first "I Love NY HH Hostel" in Cortand NY back in July of 2003.
You are free to look through the new logbook but please do not take a
hitchhiker unless you have one to share. I myself prefer to find HHs
"in the wild" but hostels are a great way to get your HH's further out
there and to see stamp carvers from all over. Remember: TAKE a
Hitchhiker - LEAVE a Hitchhiker. I have located it as close as I can
to major thoughfares to encourage out of towners to visit and share
HH's. The ROUND TRIP drive time from the thruway is less than 15
minutes..and from 81 north and south it is less than 5 minutes. So, no
excuses not to stop!!

Even if you don't have a HH to share, please visit and stamp into the
Hostel logbook and collect the stamp. This counts as a traditional
letterbox find. Also, I would like to publicly thank Celtic Quinn for
donating 3 of the HH's to "seed" the hostel.

I have been very busy doing maintenance on my 100+ boxes. Some missing
boxes will probably be retired..and some replaced. Thank you for
everyone who sends me status reports. I am checking each box
personally before I render a verdict. I appreciate all of your patience.

Buh Buh Buh Bennie has been found and put back in his ORIGINAL spot.
The clues have been changed to reflect this. I will be posting a
status report of my boxes, thus far for those who are interested.

Report from the Road

From: Cyndie | Date: 2006-06-26 12:33:00 UTC
I had the craziest weekend. I spent it doing maintenance on my boxes
and discovered a grouping that was completely missing. Not just one
or one series, but 2 series and a couple of singles..all within one
square mile. I don't know what is up, but I have decided to retire
the two series, and wait and rehide the singles in slightly
different spots. Weird, huh?

What's even weirder is what happened later. I got home and was SOOO
drained of energy. My right wrist looked swollen (the one without
the tattoo) and I was getting really concerned. As my energy level
went lower and lower, I decided to just put away my things and make
an early night of it.

Well, you wouldn't believe what I found when I emptied my black
boxing bag! You might have seen me with this bag before..it's from
the film festival I work for and is great for boxing.I stuck my hand
in to get any last leaves or stray markertops and pulled out a
cootie!!! And not just ANY cootie..but a Celtic Quinn Cootie!! It
was round with 6 legs with distinctive Celtic markings on it's back.
It tried to scuttle away but I grabbed it and held it fast for
closer examination. And then the unthinkable happened! It squirted
gas from its butt...which rose like some sort of pagan smoke
ring...until it formed the letters..... "C"....."Q". I screamed and
dropped it so quickly that I think it was stunned...as it could
barely drag itself away. A little braver now that it was wounded, I
picked it up and quickly realized my error. It WASN'T wounded...it
was only pretending! With a wink and a mischievous grin, it dove
back into my black bag, where it attached itself to the fabric.

"Ho HO," I muttered. "It's not going to get off THAT easily!" I
vowed to get rid of this Puck-like character as soon as possible! My
mind raced as I tried to think where I could have encountered such a
horrible thing. My only guess is at the Ithaca/Finger Lakes gather,
for I have seen scarce boxers since that event. I spent the rest of
the weekend rubbing my right wrist cootie-bite and planning my next
move. And exacting my revenge on the originator of this horrid
little creature...
