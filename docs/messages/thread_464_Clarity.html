*sigh* Here I go again. I originally SUGGESTED we think about merging
WNY and CNY to into a bigger LBNY as I thought it would be more
inclusive and we could make the state of NY a boxing mecca as much as
Connecticut has made their's. After all, we have the biggest
wilderness east of the Mississippi as well as wonderful lakes and one
of the biggest and grandest cities in the WORLD. Heck, we even have a
thousand islands!! :) Fat chance we'll be tripping over boxes anytime
soon in THIS state.

I DO foresee a time when this hobby gets SO big, that it will
splinter into many different groups..just like LBNA became regions,
and regions have become states. I just didn't think we were to that
point. You can drive anywhere in the state in under 6 hours and we
have less than 100 members in ALL of LBNY...even less in the old CNY
and WNY. AQ has state boards, without even regional ones and it seems
to work just fine. I don't see anyone clamoring to Ryan that we
divide the states up..or even group the states together to have
regions. At least, not yet.

But I want to reiterate that mine was a SUGGESTION only. After that
it went out of my hands and was switched overnight. A painful change,
but there was less bitching after it than anyone thought. Only a
handful of people complained...including me because members were
never allowed to vote on it.

But what I really enjoy is boxing, boxing, boxing and more boxing. :)

From: Daina | Date: 2006-02-12 21:47:51 UTC
In Lock Wench's defense, I should definitely mention that I was the
one who closed up the WNY boards, not her. After I asked about
changing the LBWNY logo to reflect all of NY last summer, she
mentioned that she and the LBCNY group had met that summer to talk
about how to include more people in the CNY group. After she made the
suggestion about being one unified state, quite a few people said
that they felt having one board would be easier. That led me to
believe that we in WNY might really have been quietly offending
others by starting our own group, so I wanted to remedy that and
QUICK ('cause Lord KNOWS I didn't want to make boxing enemies at ANY
time!)

What I didn't take into account was two things: One, I should have
realized that the opinions of those in CNY about WNY's group weren't
nearly as important as those in WNY itself. I know some people in CNY
feel like they should have had the chance to vote as to whether or
not to keep WNY, but since we weren't a single group back then, that
wouldn't really have made sense (like New Yorkers voting to keep Ohio
or something!). What should have actually happened was each group
should have voted on whether or not to merge with the other in the
privacy of their own talk boards. Then if both groups agreed, we
should have closed both CNY and WNY, then started a brand new group
with the same owners and moderators of both groups to call LBNY. That
way, it would have been more of a merge instead of an annexing of one
group or the other.

I really do feel badly about closing the group without thinking this
through, but to tell you the truth, I really felt under pressure to
do this when it seemed that a lot of people agreed with Lock Wench's
suggestion (for various reasons, all good ones to boot). Plus, the
idea that CNY folks might come to hate WNY folks over our little
glorified e-mail list was just too much to shoulder. I certainly
didn't want to offend anyone, but in trying to please all, I forgot
to take into account the second thing, which is the fact that what's
said on the boards isn't always general consensus. Some folks don't
have the time or mental energy to waste on standing up for what they
want on the boards (heck, I know I get worn out and shaky writing
these long posts)!, so contrary to popular belief, there was a LOT of
bitching about it that went on later, just not on the boards. People
came up to me at gatherings or sent me personal e-mail to say they
felt bad that I had closed WNY and wished they'd been there to
provide a second opinion or viewpoint. I think the only person who
had time to post an opposing view from WNY was ROCnRon...and then his
computer crashed!

I don't think I ever doubted that having a statewide board would be
good, but, but as catbead said, I do miss the comraderie of the
smaller group (which essentially consisted of me and Celtic Quinn
posting goofy stuff back and forth to folks who had limited computer
access in Buffalo and Rochester!).
